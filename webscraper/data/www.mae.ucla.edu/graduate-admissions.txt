				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p><span style="font-size: 14px">Thank you for your interest in the Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Department Graduate Programs. Please review our admissions requirements before contacting us, for many questions can be answered by reading the instructions, reading the</span><a style="font-size: 14px" href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/faqs/"> UCLA Graduate Education Admissions FAQs</a><span style="font-size: 14px">, as well as the MAE department&#8217;s Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) at the bottom of this page.</span></p>
<p>Due to the high volume of inquiries, applicants will not receive a personalized response. In recent years, we have found that 97% of the responses to these inquiries can be found on our FAQ admission webpage or the <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/faqs/">UCLA Graduate Education Admissions FAQs</a>. Most questions can be answered by simply reviewing or searching a topic, such as GRE, TOEFL, official transcripts, personal statement,  and document status on the above webpages.</p>
<p>Unfortunately, we will not answer hypothetical questions. In the rare instance when a question is not answered on one of the above webpages or in the general response below, a personalized response may be provided. As noted in the above webpages, applicants are asked <u>not </u>to make inquiries about the receipt of documents. Doing so will delay our efforts to process application material.</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px">The</span><strong style="font-size: 14px"> Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Department </strong><span style="font-size: 14px">has been in existence as a separate entity since 1984. It has a faculty of 34 full-time professors, all of whom serve as Graduate Advisers to our 335 graduate students. All our </span><a style="font-size: 14px" href="https://samueli.ucla.edu/search-faculty/#mae" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">faculty</a><span style="font-size: 14px"> are engaged in research projects, usually externally funded, that produce leading contributions to engineering and science.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px">Please note that admission to the department is quite competitive. Recently admitted applicants have had a mean grade point average of 3.67/4.00, while their Graduate Record Examination (GRE) scores on the Verbal, Quantitative, and Analytical sections have averaged 79%, 92%, 84% respectively. (Note that the GRE is not required for 2023.)</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px">The UCLA </span><a style="font-size: 14px" href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/applicat.htm"><strong><em>Application for Graduate Admission</em></strong></a><span style="font-size: 14px"> should be used to apply. The online application includes complete instructions and further information.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px">University regulations do not permit an applicant to apply to more than one major. Please decide which of our programs is most appropriate for you prior to submitting an application. Applicants with existing MS or PhD degrees in Mechanical Engineering or Aerospace Engineering or closely related areas will not be admitted for a duplicate degree at the same level.</span></p>
<p>Those admitted to the Ph.D. program without a master’s degree are required to complete the UCLA M.S. in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering while proceeding toward the Ph.D.</p>
<p>Students who already hold a M.S. or Ph.D in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering or in a closely related field will not be admitted for a duplicate degree at the same level.  Furthermore, after entering the program, no change in degree objective will be approved that would result in the granting of a duplicate degree at a level for which a student has an existing degree in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering or closely related field**.</p>
<p><em>**Fields considered closely related are: other disciplines of engineering, computer science, physics, and mathematics. </em></p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>CONTACT</strong></p>
<div dir="ltr">
<div dir="ltr">
<p>Students <em>and </em>Prospective students, please contact Abel Lebon at (310) 825-7780 (<a href="mailto:abel@seas.ucla.edu">abel@seas.ucla.edu</a>).</p>
<p>Please allow 5-10 business days to receive a response.</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px">Finally, <strong>please refrain from contacting the department to verify if materials have been received.</strong> The Student Affairs Office will email you if your application is incomplete or if any documents are missing. However, ensure that all required materials have been submitted or uploaded before submission of your application. The applicant assumes responsibility to document that all required materials have been submitted (for example, through receipts and screenshots).</span></p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>DEADLINES</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Department will consider applications for Fall Quarter admission only. Due to the number of applications received this deadline is strictly observed.</p>
<p><strong>Submission Period:</strong><br />September 15 &#8211; December 1</p>
<p>Note that applications submitted before this time period will be directed to the previous application cycle, and therefore, will not be considered nor reviewed.</p>
<p><strong>Annual Deadline:</strong><br />December 1, 23:59 (PST)</p>

<p><strong>Check Application Status:</strong><br />The uploading of documents on the UCLA <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/applicat.htm">Application for Graduate Admission </a>signifies the receipt of application materials.</p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>APPLICATION FEE</strong></p>
<p>The nonrefundable application fee is $135.00 for U.S. Citizens and Permanent Residents and $155.00 for all other applicants. UCLA accepts Visa, MasterCard, and Discover.</p>
<p>For questions about fee waivers, review carefully the guidelines established by UCLA. Refrain from contacting the department to request fee waivers. You must follow the instructions outlined on the <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/faqs/">UCLA Graduate Education</a>. This includes uploading all required documents by the deadline. Failure to upload required documents, uploading illegible documents, uploading documents without redacting social security numbers, or otherwise not following instructions will cause your fee waiver to be rejected.</p>
<p>Note that although individual departments verify whether an applicant meets the <span class="il">fee</span> <span class="il">waiver</span> requirements, departments can <em>only <span class="il">waive</span> fees if applicants meet the criteria established by UCLA</em>. Please see <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/faqs/"><span class="il">Fee</span> Waivers: Need-based on Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)</a></p>
<ul>
<li>Cash, checks, or money orders <strong>cannot</strong> be accepted at UCLA.</li>
<li>The application fee must be paid before an application can be considered.</li>
<li>The application fee is devoted to the UCLA administrative cost of processing all applications received, and is <strong>not</strong> <strong>refundable</strong> under any circumstances, regardless of the outcome, the date of filing, time of review, or if, for whatever the reason, the application is withdrawn.</li>
</ul>
<p>Shortly after receiving your application, Diversity, Inclusion and Admissions sends an acknowledgment via email. Applicants should expect to receive an email acknowledgment within 72 hours of <strong>submitting</strong> the online application.</p>
<p>If you do not receive an email acknowledgment within a reasonable amount of time (five to seven days), please review your online application to be sure the submission process was completed. Please also be sure to check your &#8220;junk&#8221; email box, in case UCLA&#8217;s email was diverted there by your spam filter. If you still have not received the acknowledgment email, please contact <a href="mailto:onlineapphelp@grad.ucla.edu">onlineapphelp@grad.ucla.edu</a> in case there is a minor problem with your application data.</p>
<p>When a decision is made, applicants receive an email, directing them to the Graduate Division&#8217;s application site and the option Decision Status. Applicants sign in using the email address used to create their application account (<em>not</em> the User Name) and their application password to read the official decision letter.</p>
<p>If admitted, the conditions of admission and information and instructions concerning registration (via a link to the Statement of Intent to Register and a Statement of Legal Residence) will be included as part of the offer of admission letter, which can be printed out from the Graduate Division site. (International applicants: please read <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/VISAPROC.HTM">Visa Procedures/Financial Verification</a>.)</p>
<p>Only official notification from the Dean of the Graduate Division constitutes approval of admission.</p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>TRANSCRIPTS</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left">Please carefully review the requirements for <em>Official</em> and <em>Unofficial</em> transcripts here:  <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/materials-to-be-uploaded-or-sent-to-the-departmentprogramschool/">https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/materials-to-be-uploaded-or-sent-to-the-departmentprogramschool/</a>.</p>
<p style="text-align: left">We define an<em> unofficial transcript</em> as either an official electronic transcript or an official paper transcript that you (the applicant) have scanned, uploaded or emailed to us.</p>
<p style="text-align: left">Although we no longer require submission of official transcripts at the time of your application, transcripts <em>must</em> contain certain information, as specified below and here: https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/materials-to-be-uploaded-or-sent-to-the-departmentprogramschool/.  <strong><em>Improper transcripts can delay the admissions process by several months, and the application will be considered low priority.</em> </strong></p>
<p>Every applicant is required to upload an unofficial transcript on the <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/applicat.htm">online Graduate Admissions </a>website.  Please review acceptable transcripts here: <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/materials-to-be-uploaded-or-sent-to-the-departmentprogramschool/">https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/materials-to-be-uploaded-or-sent-to-the-departmentprogramschool/</a>.)</p>
<p>Note that the transcript must be a document created by the institution and must show the following:</p>
<p>1. name of the applicant</p>
<p>2. the dates of enrollment</p>
<p>3. the subjects or courses taken</p>
<p>4. the units of credit or time allotted to each subject</p>
<p>5. the name of the institution</p>
<p>6. a complete description of the institution&#8217;s grading scale or other standard of evaluation</p>
<p><strong>Tip:</strong> Because of the numerous applicants who upload unacceptable transcripts, we recommend that you request an <em>official</em> transcript from your institution to upload to your application. This should avoid issues in processing your application for admission.  We define an unofficial transcript as either an official electronic transcript or an official paper transcript that you (the applicant) have scanned, uploaded or emailed to us. All documents uploaded by the applicant (i.e., transcripts, GRE, or TOEFL/IELTS scores) are considered <strong><em>unofficial</em></strong>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>All applicants who have been recommended for admission and who have accepted our offer are required to submit an official transcript from their institution.</p>
<p>All official transcripts must bear the actual seal and/or signature of the Registrar. Photocopies and screen shots are not considered official transcripts. <strong>Do not send the original of an academic record which cannot be replaced</strong>; obtain a properly certified copy instead. All transcripts and credentials are evaluated by the UCLA Graduate Admissions Office in terms of UCLA equivalency.</p>
<p><em>International students: Please read the <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/required-academic-records/">Graduate Division’s Required Academic Records</a> information prior to submitting your transcripts. You may need to submit supplemental documents such as degree certificates and/or diplomas, or transcripts in the original language of instruction. Failure to submit the <a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/required-academic-records/">Required Academic Records</a> will delay the processing of your application.</em></p>
<p>Entering students are expected to have completed the B.S. degree requirements with at least a 3.0/4.0 cumulative grade-point average of undergraduate study.</p>
<p>If you are a university/college senior, then do not wait for senior-year grades before submitting your application and transcript.</p>
<p>If you have completed your B.S. degree when you are ready to submit your application, then you must include your final transcript.</p>
<p>Students entering the Ph.D. program are expected to have completed the requirements for the master&#8217;s degree with at least a 3.25/4.0 grade-point average. Normally the M.S. degree is required for admission to the Ph.D. program. Exceptional students, however, can be admitted to the Ph.D. program without having an M.S. degree but would be required to complete the M.S. degree along the way.</p>
<p>When filling out your application, fill out your grades in the scale that your institution uses. Do not attempt to translate your grades yourself, as we have a specialist in the department who will translate them. Meeting the above minimum requirements does not guarantee admission.</p>
<p>Applicants with existing MS or PhD degrees in Mechanical Engineering or Aerospace Engineering or closely related areas will not be admitted for a duplicate degree at the same level.</p>
<p>UCLA undergraduates please note:  Although you are required to upload your UCLA transcript online, you do not have to submit an official copy of UCLA transcripts to our office.</p>

Once these addresses have been submitted, each recommender will be notified electronically by UCLA, and they will then submit their letters of recommendation using a secure interface.</p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>STATEMENT OF PURPOSE</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The online application includes a section in which you must upload your “Statement of Purpose&#8221;. You will be required to describe exactly what you wish to study. This statement should be precise and brief. Your statement is a means of helping us match your academic and research interests with those of our faculty (this is very important if you are applying for financial support). It should not be a personal life history; it should contain only such personal details that will aid the Admissions Committee in evaluating your application. Describe exactly what you wish to study at UCLA. &#8220;Mechanical Engineering&#8221;, for example is not specific enough. State what branch of Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering interests you, i,e., structural and solid mechanics, systems and control, heat and mass transfer, etc. For more information regarding specific fields of study in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering, see Graduate Research Areas.</p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>TOEFL EXAMINATION</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Students whose native language is not English are required to take and pass the <a href="http://www.toefl.org/">TOEFL Examination</a>.</p>
<p>A TOEFL score of at least 560 on the paper and pencil test or at least 87 on the internet-based test is the minimum required for applicants to graduate programs at UCLA. For the internet-based TOEFL (TOEFL iBT), the following are recommended minimum passing scores for each section: Writing: 25, Speaking: 24, Reading: 21, Listening: 17, but the required minimum total passing score: 87.  An IELTS overall band score of at least 7.0 is the minimum required.</p>
<p>To avoid errors, please ensure that your name on your application matches <em>exactly</em> with the name that you enter on your exam, including hyphens and spaces.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Institution Code = 4837 </strong><br /><strong>Department Code = 68 </strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Please note that TOEFL scores are considered valid for only two (2) years of the start of the intended application term, and that if several scores are submitted, only the most recent is considered.</p>
<p>Admitted applicants are required, upon arrival at UCLA, to take the UCLA <a href="http://www.wp.ucla.edu/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=114&amp;Itemid=101">English as a Second Language </a><a href="http://www.wp.ucla.edu/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=114&amp;Itemid=101">Placement Examination (ESLPE), </a>an English diagnostic test, before being permitted to register. Depending on the results of the ESLPE, you may be required to complete English as a Second Language courses beginning in your first term at UCLA. If English courses are required, you should anticipate spending a longer period of time at the University. Admission is canceled for those who do not pass the ESLPE.  Please note that you must sit for the ESLPE <strong>regardless</strong> of your score obtained on the TOEFL, IELTS or any other English proficiency test.</p>
<p>Applicants who hold a bachelor’s or higher degree from a university located in the United States or in another country in which English is both the primary spoken language of daily life (e.g., Australia, Barbados, Canada, Ireland, Jamaica, New Zealand, United Kingdom) <em>and</em> the medium of instruction, or who have completed at least two years of full-time study at such an institution, are exempted from both the <em>TOEFL/IELTS</em> requirement and the <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/ENGREQ.HTM#ESLPE">English as a Second Language Placement Examination (ESLPE)</a>. Please be sure to include such information in your application.</p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>FINANCIAL SUPPORT</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Research Assistantship</strong><br />Graduate student research positions are awarded directly by individual faculty. This is the most common form of financial assistance. Faculty members obtain contracts from various government agencies and industry to carry out research in applied topics. A portion of these funds are meant to pay trained graduate students a salary to assist in research which will normally result in a thesis for the student and scientific progress in the topic. Research Assistantships are individually negotiated between each student and his/her thesis advisor.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Fellowships</strong><br />Exceptional Fall applicants, and in some cases continuing students, are offered fellowships which include a stipend and tuition expense. These awards are typically for one year, and the total monetary compensation is approximately equivalent to a Teaching Assistantship or Research Assistantship.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Teaching Assistantship</strong><br />Teaching Assistantships are reserved primarily for Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering graduate students. Preference is usually given to doctorate students. Applicants from abroad who wish to apply for TA positions should take the Test of Spoken English (TSE) at the TOEFL Center in their home country or the equivalent TOP exam given at UCLA.</p>

<p style="text-align: center"><strong>REQUIREMENTS FOR INTERNATIONAL APPLICANTS</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Information about obtaining the I-20 Certificate of Eligibility will be made available to admitted applicants by the <a href="http://www.internationalcenter.ucla.edu/">Dashew Center for International Students and Scholars</a>. Admitted applicants requiring a DS2019 for a J1 visa should contact the program to which they have been admitted to submit the request.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>U.S. immigration law requires that international applicants, if admitted, show documented evidence that sufficient funds to cover all tuition, fees, transportation, and living expenses are available for the first year of their studies at UCLA. This must be proved before a Certificate of Eligibility (<em>I-20</em> or <em>DS2019</em>) for a visa can be issued.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The I-20 form is issued to admitted applicants who apply, and are qualified, for an F-1 (student) visa, after they submit a Statement of Intent to Register accepting admission at UCLA.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The DS2019 form is issued to admitted applicants who apply, and are qualified for, a J-1 visa, after they submit a Statement of Intent to Register accepting admission at UCLA. J-1 (exchange visitor) status is appropriate for students whose programs of study are substantially funded by the U.S. government, their home government, an international or nonprofit organization, or UCLA.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Since the University cannot assume financial responsibility for its students, U.S. government regulations require that we be satisfied that entering students have sufficient funds to meet all expenses while at UCLA. Be aware that all fees and expenses are subject to change; <em>it is best to plan to have additional funding well beyond the minimum.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Unless you have been officially notified in writing of an award from the department or the Graduate Division, you should not expect any financial support from UCLA. Teaching assistantships, graduate student research appointments, fellowships and stipends, as well as tuition fellowships, are not guaranteed for the full academic year or for subsequent years of study, unless stated in writing.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>A student on a student visa cannot accept off-campus employment at any time during the first year in the U.S. Spouses of F-1 visa holders (F-2) are not allowed to work under any circumstances.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Students transferring from another U.S. college or university must request a transfer to UCLA from their current school, and must also complete Immigration and Naturalization Service requirements before the end of the second week of their first term at UCLA. To comply, students must report to the <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/OISS.HTM">Dashew Center for International Students and Scholars</a> and bring a letter from the former school indicating that the student maintained visa status and is eligible to transfer to UCLA.</p>

<h1>Graduate Admissions Frequently Asked Questions</h1>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Here you will find answers to <em>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)</em> about Admissions into the Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Graduate Programs.</p>
<p>In addition, please review our instructions for submitting your application <em>before</em> contacting us for more information: https://www.mae.ucla.edu/graduate-admissions/<br />&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8211;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>APPLICATION/ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS AND DATA<br />Q1. <strong>Does the department provide “pre-application” reviews?</strong><br />A1. Unfortunately, we do not pre-review your application. Prospective applicants should review this page, frequently asked questions, and the department’s annual report to determine their admission competitiveness, and academic and research fit with the university. We conduct a comprehensive and thorough review of applications and consider many factors when issuing an admission decision.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Q2. <strong>Do I need to have everything ready before I begin my application?</strong><br />A2. No. In fact, we encourage applicants to start their application early to familiarize themselves with the process. The applicant is able to begin, save, and update their application before submitting. However, it is important to submit the completed application with all necessary documents and information by the December 1 deadline.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Q3. <strong>What are the application requirements?<br /></strong>A3. An applicant for admission must hold a bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university, and must have the necessary academic preparation to complete graduate level courses in MAE. The university requires at least a scholastic average of B (3.0 on a 4.0 scale) or its equivalent if the letter grade system is not used. International students must submit a TOEFL with a total score of at least 87 (computer-based) or 560 (paper-based), or an IELTS with a score of at least 7.0.</p>
<p>See the section <strong>APPLICATION MATERIAL REQUIRED </strong>for additional MAE department document requirements.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Q4. <strong>Do you accept applications for admission for study beginning winter or spring quarter?</strong><br />A4. No. The MAE department accepts applications for fall quarter only. Applications should be submitted only between mid-September and the deadline of December 1.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Q5. <strong>My academic background is not in mechanical and aerospace engineering. Am I eligible to apply to your graduate program?</strong><br />A5. The vast majority of applicants have a background in MAE. Other majors represented include electrical engineering, bioengineering, and physics. An applicant without a MAE background may still be a very competitive applicant. Having professional experience as well as having taken courses that are equivalent to prerequisites for upper division undergraduate coursework, and the upper division courses themselves, would be most helpful. Applicants may illustrate their professional experience by succinctly describing their skills and projects in the statement of purpose, and submitting letters of recommendation that relate their achievements and skills to their potential for success in their degree and career objectives.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

Through petition, courses completed in graduate status on other UC campuses may apply to master’s programs at UCLA, provided they were not used toward a previous degree. <p>Q5. <strong>My university does not assign grades on a 4.0 grade scale. Should I calculate my GPA?<br /></strong>A5. No. Staff in the MAE graduate student affairs office review and convert, as necessary, the marks or grades of all applicants. The Graduate Division online application asks applicants to submit their grade point average on the scale used by their institutions.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Q6. <strong>My official transcripts do not contain all the requested information such as the month and year of award. Do I need to submit a diploma?<br /></strong>A6. A complete academic history must include attested or certified copies of official documents that provide the following: 1) individual subjects or classes with marks or grades; 2) class placement or overall grade; 3) degree certificate that includes degree received and degree conferral date; 4) institutional marks/grading scale; and 5) the original language of instruction and English translation.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Most applicants are in the process of completing a degree. If offered admission, the final transcript indicating the date of the degree conferral may be submitted during the summer. Applicants from China, India, Iran, as well as most other countries (exceptions include Great Britain, Hong Kong, Korea, Taiwan) who have graduated must submit official copies of their degree certificates along with their final official transcripts. Both the original language of instruction and English translation must be submitted.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>VISA (INTERNATIONAL APPLICANTS ONLY)<br />Q1. <strong>How much funding must I provide to qualify for a visa to study in the United States?</strong><br />A1. The total amount of money needed for living and educational expenses for the 2017-2018 academic year for most programs at UCLA, including mechanical and aerospace, is about US $52,000. This includes 1) US about $31,100 for tuition and fees for three quarters, and 2) US about $19,500 for basic living costs (food, rent, laundry, local transportation, books and supplies, etc.). It is estimated that the cost for the 2017-2018 academic year will be at least $52,500. To qualify for a visa to study in the U.S., international applicants must demonstrate personal or agency financial support of at least $52,500 for the first year. Prospective students should apply for admission ONLY if they can provide such documentation, which will be requested if admission is offered, in early March. Information on the cost of attendance is available on the <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/expenses.htm">UCLA Graduate Division Cost of Attendance webpage</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Information about university-wide fellowships and grants, and external fellowships may be found at the <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/asis/stusup/index.html">UCLA Graduate Division funding webpage</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Q2. <strong>When may I apply for a visa?</strong><br />A2. The UCLA Dashew Center for International Students and Scholars prepares visa documents (Form I-20 for F-1 student visa) from May to September for admitted applicants who have returned the Statement of Intent to Register, Statement of Legal Residence, and CFS that includes the appropriate financial documentation. Please see information on visa procedures on the <a href="http://www.gdnet.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/VISAPROC.HTM">Graduate Division website</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p>WRITING SAMPLE<br />Q1. <strong>Is a writing sample required?</strong><br />A1. The statement of purpose is considered to be the writing sample. No additional writing sample is requested. You may upload a blank document or your SOP as your personal history statement.</p>
