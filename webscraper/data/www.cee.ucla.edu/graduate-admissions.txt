				<div class="et_pb_tab_content"><h3 id="DecideOnAProgram"><strong>Decide on a Program</strong></h3>
<p>The Civil and Environmental Engineering department offers graduate programs in the following sub-disciplines:</p>
<ul>
<li>Civil Engineering Materials (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
<li>Environmental Engineering (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
<li>Geotechnical Engineering (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
<li>Hydrology and Water Resources (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
<li>Structures and Civil Engineering Materials (M.S.)</li>
<li>Structural/Earthquake Engineering (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
<li>Structural Mechanics (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
<li>Transportation Engineering (M.S., Ph.D.)</li>
</ul>
<p>Please take a moment to explore <strong><a href="https://www.cee.ucla.edu/research" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">these areas</a></strong> before applying. To view program details and requisite information, please see our<strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/programs/school-of-engineering-and-applied-science/civil-environmental-engineering-department/civil-engineering/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Program Requirements</a></strong>. Course descriptions can be found in the <strong><a href="http://www.registrar.ucla.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">General Catalog</a></strong>.</p>
<p>University regulations do not permit an applicant to apply to more than one major field or more than one degree program. Please decide which of our programs is most appropriate for you prior to submitting an application.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>The 2024-2025 application deadline is <span style="color: #ff0000">December 15, 2023</span>.</strong></h3>
<p><strong><span>Apply Here:</span><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/admission-application-for-graduate-admission/"><b><u>Admission: Application for Graduate Admission | UCLA Graduate Programs</u></b></a></strong></p>

<p>For more financial aid information, please <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/asis/stusup/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">click here</a></strong>.</p></div>

				<div class="et_pb_tab_content"><h3 id="OnlineApplication"><strong>Online Application</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">When submitting your application, please make sure you fully complete the submission process. You should receive an acknowledgment email from UCLA Graduate Division within 3 days of submission. (Check your &#8220;junk&#8221; email folder as well!) If, after 3 days, you have not received an acknowledgment email, please contact <a href="mailto:onlineapphelp@grad.ucla.edu"><strong>onlineapphelp@grad.ucla.edu</strong></a>.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Helpful notes for the application:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Specify M.S. or Ph.D. in Civil Engineering and intended field of study (Environmental, Hydrology and Water Resources, Geotechnical, Structures, or Transportation).</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Fill out your grade point average(GPA) using your institution&#8217;s scale. Do not attempt to translate your GPA yourself; we will translate them when reviewing your application.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Use your name as it appears on your passport. If the name on your passport does not match the name on your application documents (transcripts, test scores, etc.), fill out the Other Name(s) section with your name as it appears on those documents.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">The email address indicated on the online application will be the one used to communicate with you throughout the application process.</span></li>
</ul>
<h3 id="ApplicationFee"><strong>Application Fee</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">U.S. Citizens and Permanent Residents: $135.00</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">All other applicants: $155.00</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">UCLA accepts Visa, MasterCard, and Discover. Cash, checks, and money orders are not accepted. The application fee is not refundable under any circumstances, regardless of admissions decision, date of filing, time of review, or application withdrawal. Fee waiver information can be found on the<span> </span></span><strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/research-requirements/">UCLA Graduate Education website</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400">.</span></p>
<h3 id="MinimumDegreeGradeRequirements"><strong>Minimum Degree and Grade Requirements</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">All U.S. student applicants&#8217; degrees must be from regionally accredited institutions and comparable to ones from the University of California.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">M.S. applicants:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Required to hold a Bachelor&#8217;s degree with a minimum 3.0 GPA on a 4.0 scale (or equivalent).</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Ph.D. applicants:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Required to hold a Bachelor&#8217;s degree with a minimum 3.0 GPA on a 4.0 scale (or equivalent).</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Strongly encouraged to hold a Master&#8217;s degree with a minimum 3.25 GPA on a 4.0 scale (or equivalent).</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">International students:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Post-secondary degrees completed outside the U.S. must represent completion of at least four years of study with above average scholarship from a university or university-level institution.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Please read Graduate Division&#8217;s </span><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/ACADRECS.HTM"><strong>Required Academic Records</strong></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> prior to submitting your transcripts. You may need to submit supplemental documents such as degree certificates and/or diplomas. Failure to submit the Required Academic Records will delay the processing of your application.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Helpful notes:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">If you are a university/college senior, do not wait for senior-year grades before submitting your application and transcript.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">If you are a UCLA undergraduate, you do not have to submit official copies of UCLA transcripts. However, please upload an unofficial transcript to your online application.</span></li>
</ul>
<h3 id="SubmittingTranscripts"><strong>Submitting Transcripts</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li><span>Please upload</span><span><strong>unofficial copies</strong> of your transcripts and degree certificates/diploma (if applicable) </span><span>to your application. An official, mailed transcript and degree certificate/diploma (if applicable) is not required unless you are admitted.</span></li>
<li><span>I</span><span>f you are currently earning a degree</span><strong><span>outside the U.S.</span></strong><span>, please upload your unofficial transcript and degree certificate/diploma (if applicable) in both <strong>English and the original language</strong>.</span></li>
</ul>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h3 id="StatementOfPurpose"><strong>Statement of Purpose</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">A Statement of Purpose should succinctly describe your interest in our program, your intended field of study, specific areas of interest within said field, future plans, and other aspects of your background which may help us evaluate your application. The Statement of Purpose is also used to match admitted students with assigned advisors. Please be as specific as possible in your Statement of Purpose.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Please upload your Statement of Purpose using the online application. Your Statement of Purpose should be 500 words or fewer.</span></p>
<h3 id="PersonalStatement"><strong>Personal Statement </strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The Personal Statement is an opportunity for you to provide additional information that may help us in evaluating your preparation and aptitude for graduate study in our program. You are </span><strong>required</strong><span style="font-weight: 400"> to complete a Personal Statement; an application without one is considered incomplete.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">If you would like to be considered for the </span><strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/funding/financial-aid/funding-for-entering-students/eugene-v-cota-robles-fellowship/">Eugene Cota-Robles</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400"> and</span><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/funding/financial-aid/funding-for-entering-students/graduate-opportunity-fellowship-program-gofp/"> <span style="font-weight: 400"><strong>Graduate Opportunity</strong></span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> fellowships, please address one or more of the following prompts:</span></p>

<p><strong>What are the minimum GPA and GRE requirements for admission?</strong> Our programs require a Bachelors degree from a regionally accredited institution, comparable in standard to a Bachelors degree from the University of California. In general, successful applicants have demonstrated outstanding quantitative ability and proven success in previous academic endeavors, particularly coursework relevant to their intended major. Applicants to our M.S. programs are expected to hold a Bachelors degree with a minimum grade point average of 3.0 or equivalent. Applicants to our Ph.D. programs should have completed a Master&#8217;s degree program with a 3.25 minimum grade point average, and hold a Bachelors degree with a 3.0 minimum grade point average (or equivalent). We do not have a minimum GRE score requirement for admission. We conduct a comprehensive and thorough review of applications and consider many factors when issuing an admission decision.</p>
<p><strong>Can I apply if I do not have an undergraduate degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering?</strong> In rare circumstances, applicants without an undergraduate degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering may be admitted to our graduate programs. Typically these applicants have related degrees who have completed requisite coursework. For example, an Environmental Science undergraduate major applying to our Environmental and Water Resources Engineering graduate program who has also completed requisites for the graduate degree. We encourage applicants to complete requisite coursework for their intended graduate degree program prior to applying. Requisite requirements may be found in our <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/programs/school-of-engineering-and-applied-science/civil-environmental-engineering-department/civil-engineering/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Program Requirements</a></strong>. Course descriptions may be found in the <strong><a href="https://catalog.registrar.ucla.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Catalog</a></strong>.</p>
<p><strong>Can I apply for a M.S. program if I already have a M.S. degree?</strong> The University of California, in general discourages the duplication of advanced degrees. All applicants for a second academic graduate degree at the same level as, or at a lower level than the graduate degree they already hold, will be required to show compelling cause to the department before it reviews and recommends admission to the Dean of the Graduate Division. If admitted for a second graduate degree, students will be held to all the usual degree requirements and University regulations pertaining to fees, registrations, examinations, advancement to candidacy, residency, etc. Courses already applied to any earlier graduate degree cannot be applied to the second degree.</p>
<p><strong>I am a current M.S./Ph.D. student. Can I transfer to your institution?</strong> We do not have a separate transfer admission process at the graduate level. All applicants submit the same graduate application. M.S. applicants should also refer to the M.S. transfer coursework policy by reviewing the <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/gasaa/library/spfgs.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Standards and Procedures for Graduate Study at UCLA</a></strong>.</p>
<p><strong>I have already taken some graduate level coursework. Will I be able to transfer the coursework if admitted to the M.S. program?</strong> M.S. applicants should refer to the M.S. transfer coursework policy by reviewing the <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/gasaa/library/spfgs.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Standards and Procedures for Graduate Study at UCLA</a></strong>. Through petition, courses completed in graduate status on other UC campuses may apply to masters programs at UCLA, provided they were not used toward a previous degree. Such courses may fulfill up to one-half of the total course requirement, one-half of the graduate course requirement, and one-third of the academic residence requirement. A maximum of two courses completed with a minimum grade of B in graduate status at institutions other than UC may apply to UCLA masters programs. Two courses would be the equivalent of eight quarter units or five semester units. They may not fulfill the minimum five-graduate-course requirement or the academic residence requirement. The approval of the Graduate Division and the students major department is required on a petition for transfer of credit. Courses taken for any other degree previously awarded at UCLA or another institution, and courses taken before the award of the bachelors degree may not be applied toward a graduate degree at UCLA.</p>
<p><strong>What if my GRE or TOEFL/IELTS scores arrive after the application deadline? </strong>Official test scores must be received by the deadline. If materials are received after the deadline your application will be considered late. Late application may experience a delay in processing and you may not be considered for university and/or departmental funding opportunities. We will do our best to review late applications, but cannot guarantee we will be able to review for the intended application term. It is recommended that you submit all other materials prior the application deadline and your test scores will be added to your application when they are received.</p>
<p><strong>Can I submit test scores (GRE and/or TOEFL/IELTS) prior to the application deadline and submit new scores after the application deadline? What about submitting other updated application materials after the deadline? </strong></p>
<p>We will consider all scores submitted to the university. However, we cannot guarantee that a decision will not be made prior to the receipt of materials, including test scores, received after the application deadline.</p>
<p><strong>Am I eligible for a TOEFL/IELTS waiver?</strong> The UCLA <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/gasaa/admissions/ENGREQ.HTM" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Graduate Division English Requirement website</a></strong> provides an extensive explanation of who is required to submit a TOEFL/IELTS as well as exemptions.</p>
<p><strong>How do I apply for departmental funding?</strong> The Civil and Environmental Engineering department does not have a separate application process to be considered for funding. All applicants are automatically considered for departmental funding including Teaching Assistantships, Research Assistantships, and departmental fellowships. In addition, the online application has a section labeled Fellowship Application where you may select to be considered for UCLA Graduate Division Fellowships. Please be sure to visit the <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/asis/entsup/fellgrnt.htm" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Graduate Division website</a></strong> to learn more about those fellowship opportunities. Applicants are also strongly encouraged to apply for University and external funding opportunities. More information on funding is located on the <strong><a href="https://grad.ucla.edu/funding/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UCLA Graduate Divisions funding website</a></strong>. While all applicants are considered for funding, most funding opportunities are awarded to PhD applicants.</p>
<p><strong>How long does it take to earn a MS degree?</strong> Most of our students complete the MS Civil and Environmental Engineering program in one year. The Civil and Environmental Engineering MS program of study consists of at least nine graduate and upper division courses completed while in graduate status. Typically, students take three of the nine required courses every quarter.</p>
<p><strong>How long does it take to earn a PhD degree?</strong> Most students complete the Ph.D. Civil and Environmental Engineering program in 4  6 years. Applicants who are admitted into the PhD and do not hold a MS are required to complete and earn a MS degree within the first two years (6 quarters) of enrollment with UCLA. These students would then be expected to complete and earn the PhD within 5 years (15 quarters) of earning the MS. Applicants who are admitted into the PhD and hold a MS are expected to complete and earn the PhD within 5 years (15 quarters) of earning the MS.</p></div>

				<div class="et_pb_tab_content"><h2><strong>UCLA Exceptional Student Admissions Program (ESAP)</strong></h2>
<h2><em>Overview</em></h2>
<p>ESAP recognizes outstanding UCLA School of Engineering and Applied Sciences undergraduate students who wish to enter the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences graduate program upon completion of the Bachelor&#8217;s degree. ESAP students are genuine graduate students who are eligible for graduate fellowships and funding.</p>
<h2><em>Eligibility</em></h2>
<p>Please refer to the <strong><a href="http://www.seasoasa.ucla.edu/seniors/exceptional-student-admissions-program/">UCLA Engineering Office of Academic and Student Affairs</a></strong> website for ESAP eligibility. Students who meet the eligibility requirements will receive a formal notice via email informing them of their eligibility. The Civil and Environmental Engineering department will also receive notification. To maintain eligibility, students must continue to achieve the specified GPA and successfully complete the Bachelor&#8217;s degree. All ESAP students must complete the regular graduate admission application and meet the application deadline.</p>
<h2><em>Benefits</em></h2>
<p>Admission is guaranteed to the graduate program in the department in which the student earned a Bachelor&#8217;s degree for the time period specified in the ESAP letter. Civil and Environmental Engineering undergraduate students are guaranteed admission to the M.S. Civil Engineering program for three academic quarters after earning the undergraduate degree. Because our M.S. program only offers a fall intake, students should plan to apply to the graduate program during their final year of undergraduate enrollment in order to begin the program during the fall quarter following the completion of their undergraduate degree. ESAP students are not required to take the GRE. ESAP students are required to provide 2 letters of recommendation. For the 3rd letter of recommendation, please enter the name as &#8220;ESAP ESAP&#8221;, email as your own email address, ceeapply@seas.ucla.edu, or saadmin@seas.ucla.edu, and type as &#8220;Graduate Division Recommendation&#8221;. Students must meet ESAP requirements and the department must receive ESAP notification in order to receive the benefits of the ESAP program.</p>
<h2><em>Questions?</em></h2>
<p>Refer to the <strong><a href="http://www.seasoasa.ucla.edu/seniors/exceptional-student-admissions-program/">ESAP</a></strong> website. ESAP eligibility questions should be directed to Jan LaBuda (jan@seas.ucla.edu). Civil Engineering application questions should be directed to ceeapply@seas.ucla.edu.</div>
