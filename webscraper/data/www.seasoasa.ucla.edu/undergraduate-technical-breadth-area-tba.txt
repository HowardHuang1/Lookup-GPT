				</div>
				
			</div><div class="et_pb_module et_pb_toggle et_pb_toggle_0 et_pb_toggle_item  et_pb_toggle_close">
				
				
				
				
				<h2 class="et_pb_toggle_title">Faculty Executive Committee (FEC) Approved Regulations and Policies Outlining the Technical Breadth Requirement (effective Fall 2023 as of 09/25/2023)</h2>
				<div class="et_pb_toggle_content clearfix"><p><span style="font-size: medium;color: #000000"><span style="text-decoration: underline"><strong>The Technical Breadth Requirement (TBR)</strong></span>  is a 12.0 unit requirement designed to provide a student with the opportunity to gain working knowledge in a<strong> technical</strong> area <em><strong>outside their major department</strong></em>.   For example: students in the Bioengineering major cannot choose a technical breadth area (TBA) in Bioengineering.  Students must take three courses from one area.  </span><span style="color: #000000;font-size: medium">To complete the requirement with three courses, those three courses must add up to at least 12.0 units.  </span><em style="color: #000000;font-size: medium"><span style="color: #e02b20">Courses chosen to satisfy the technical breadth requirement <strong>can not be used to satisfy other degree requirements </strong>and students are responsible for meeting the requisites of courses selected. </span></em></p>
<p><span style="color: #0c71c3">Exceptions to the above are:</span></p>
<ol>
<li><span style="color: #0c71c3"> Students in the Computer Engineering major and the Computer Science and Engineering major are allowed to select any technical breadth area <em>including</em> Electrical and Computer Engineering or Computer Science.   </span></li>
<li><span style="color: #0c71c3">Students may choose a course offered by their major department IF the course is part of a schoolwide area </span><strong style="font-size: 14px;color: #0c71c3"><em><span style="color: #0c71c3">and not being used to satisfy other degree requi</span>rements</em></strong><span style="font-size: 14px;color: #0c71c3">. For example: CS majors may take COM SCI 112 for the Engineering Mathematics Area, but then may not “double count” that course to fulfill a Major Field Elective as well.</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #0c71c3">Students may take multiple department listed (M) courses as long as the course chosen is <strong><em>not used to satisfy other degree requirements.</em></strong> For example: CS major can use COM SCI M119 for the ECE TBR because it is also an ECE course but then may not “double count” that course to fulfill a Major Field Elective as well.</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #0c71c3"> Students may petition, at 6426BH, to use one lower division course to satisfy a technical breadth elective IF that lower division course is a requisite for at least one of the two upper division technical breadth courses that the student takes from the same area (and that lower division course is<strong><em> not being used to satisfy other degree requirements</em></strong>).</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #0c71c3"> Students are <span style="text-decoration: underline">not allowed</span> to use more than one course from the same subset in meeting degree requirements for any engineering major.  <span style="color: #e02b20"> </span></span><em><span style="color: #e02b20"><strong>Please refer to the SUBSET RESTRICTIONS below before selecting an area and enrolling in courses.</strong></span></em></li>
</ol></div>
