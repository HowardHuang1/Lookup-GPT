		</td><td class="right"><div id='frame_0'><p>
	Professor Leonard Kleinrock is Distinguished Professor of Computer Science at <a href="http://www.ucla.edu" target="_blank">UCLA</a>. He developed the mathematical theory of data networks, the technology underpinning the Internet, while a graduate student at <a href="http://www.mit.edu" target="_blank">MIT</a>&nbsp;in the period from 1960-1962.&nbsp; In that work, he also modeled the packetization of messages and solved for a key performance gain that packetization provides.&nbsp; The birth of the Internet occurred in his UCLA laboratory (3420 Boelter Hall) when his Host computer became the first node of the Internet in September 1969 and it was from there that he directed the transmission of <a href="/internet_first_words.html">the first message</a>&nbsp;to pass over the Internet on October 29, 1969.&nbsp;</p>
<p>
	Dr. Kleinrock received his <span style="">Ph.D.</span> from <a href="http://www.mit.edu" target="_blank">MIT</a> in 1963. He has served as a Professor of Computer Science at the <a href="http://www.ucla.edu" target="_blank">University of California, Los Angeles</a> since then, serving as Chairman of the department from 1991-1995. He received his BEE degree from <a href="http://www.ccny.cuny.edu" target="_blank">CCNY</a> in 1957. and his MS degree from MIT in 1959. He is also the recipient of a number of Honorary Doctorates. He was the first President and Co-founder of Linkabit Corporation, the co-founder of Nomadix, Inc., and Founder and Chairman of TTI/Vanguard, an advanced technology forum organization. He has published over 250 papers and authored six books on a wide array of subjects, including packet switching networks, packet radio networks, local area networks, broadband networks, gigabit networks, nomadic computing, intelligent software agents, performance evaluation, and peer-to-peer networks. During his tenure at UCLA, Dr. Kleinrock has supervised the research for over 50 Ph.D. students and numerous M.S. students. These former students now form a core group of the world&#39;s most advanced networking experts.</p>
