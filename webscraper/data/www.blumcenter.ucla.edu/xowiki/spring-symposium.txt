    </p>
    <p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;"><img align="left" height="182" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2014-2015-events/IMG_8447.JPG" width="220" /></span>

    <p>&nbsp;
    </p>
    <p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">Throughout the academic year, the UCLA Blum Center hosts a variety of educational events on campus to engage students, faculty, staff, and community members on a wide range of topics related to health, poverty, and Latin American populations. UCLA Blum Center programs have a diverse focus and are designed to engage participants through interactive and collaborative formats. To explore the inter-disciplinary nature of the study of social determinants of health for Latin American populations, the UCLA Blum Centers partners with units across campus, as well as local organizations in Los Angeles.</span>
    </p>
    <p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">Past events co-hosted by the UCLA Blum Center have included documentary screenings, panel discussions, informal student networking programs, and innovation contests. The following programs have been put on by the UCLA Blum Center to-date:</span>
    </p>
    <p>&nbsp;
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>Chicanos for Community Medicine Annual Pre-Health Conference</li>
      <li><a href="https://www.rwjf.org/sharingknowledge">2018 Robert Wood Johnson Foundation: Sharing Knowledge to Build a Culture of Health Conference</a></li>
      <li>Co-Sponsor of the <a href="http://www.laglobalhealth.org">Los Angeles Global Health Conference</a></li>
      <li><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Co-sponsor of&nbsp;<a href="http://www.iadb.org/en/events/demand-solutions/2014/demand-solutions-2014-event,9828.html" target="" title="">&quot;Demand Solutions: Ideas for Improving Quality of Life&quot;</a>&nbsp;along with the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.iadb.org/en/inter-american-development-bank,2837.html" target="" title="">Inter-American Development Bank</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a href="http://blumcenter.berkeley.edu/" target="" title="">UC Berkeley Blum Center for Developing Economies</a>. The annual conference, which is held in Washington D.C., brings together some of the most innovative and creative minds in the world to discuss and share innovative solutions that address development issues in Latin America and the Caribbean.</font></li>
      <li><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Screening of the Inter-American Development Bank-produced documentary&nbsp;<em><a href="https://vimeo.com/82323489" target="" title="">Water Everlasting?&nbsp;The Battle to Secure Haiti&#39;s Most Essential Resource</a>&nbsp;</em></font><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">followed by a panel discussion about water infrastructure and systems governance in Haiti following the 2010 earthquake.       <li><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">Screening of the documentary&nbsp;<em><a href="http://www.foodchainsfilm.com/" target="" title="">Food Chains</a>,&nbsp;</em>which exposes the exploitation of farmworkers across the US and the fight to improve working conditions. The screening was followed by a panel discussion, facilitated by UCLA Blum Center Director Michael Rodriguez, and was featured as part of the Resnick Program for Food and Law Policy&#39;s first inaugural&nbsp;<a href="https://www.law.ucla.edu/centers/social-policy/resnick-program-for-food-law-and-policy/events/881/2014/10/24/Resnick-Program-SAVE-THE-DATE-c--UCLA-Harvard-Law-School-Food-Law-and-Policy-Conference/" target="" title="">UCLA-Harvard Law School Food Law and Policy Conference</a>.&nbsp;</span></li>

  <p><strong><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Background&nbsp;</font></strong>&nbsp;
  </p>
  <p><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">The UCLA Blum Center Annual Spring Conference on Poverty and Health in Latin America is designed to advance knowledge on policy and programming solutions aimed at creating more equitable health in Latin America. The UCLA Blum Center hosted its Inaugural Spring Conference on May 1st, 2013 in DeNeve Auditorium on the UCLA campus. The theme of the conference was <em>Informing Responses to Reduce Poverty and Improve Health in Latin America</em> and the event attracted more than 150 researchers, students and medical professionals to participate in a robust program. To learn more about the events of our Inaugural Spring Conference, read <u><a href="/blumcenter/files/view/blumProgram-forWeb.pdf">the event program</a></u>.&nbsp; </font>
  </p>
  <p><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Our Second UCLA Blum Center Spring Conference, <em>Connecting International Partners to Strengthen Health Systems and Respond to Health Inequities</em>, was held May 6 &amp; 7, 2014 again in the DeNeve Auditorium.&nbsp;<span style="line-height: 21px;">The conference drew approximately 150 attendees, including health ministry and other government and community officials from 10 Latin American nations to present and exchange knowledge and practical innovations around public policy, health systems, results-based programming models, and health issues in the region. Generous supporters and collaborators of the conference included the <a href="http://www.rwjf.org/" target="" title="">Robert Wood Johnson Foundation</a>, the <a href="http://www.iadb.org/en/inter-american-development-bank,2837.html" target="" title="">Inter-American Development Bank</a> and the <a href="http://www.iadb.org/en/salud-mesoamerica-2015/salud-mesoamerica-2015-home,1904.html" target="" title="">Salud Mesoam&eacute;rica 2015 Initiative</a>. </span>You can view our photo album from the 2014 Second Annual Spring Conference <a href="/blumcenter/photos/album?album_id=23325" target="" title="">here</a>.&nbsp;</font>
