  <p align="left"><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong><font size="5">UC Blum Federation &amp; UCLA Blum Center Summer Scholars Program</font></strong> <font size="3"><strong> </strong> </font> </font>

    <p><strong><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font size="4"><u>Program Overview<img src="/blumcenter/files/view/2015-summer-scholars-headshots/escuela.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px; float: right; width: 290px; height: 215px;" /></u></font></font></strong>
    </p>
    <p>The UCLA Blum Center Summer Scholars Program is a unique opportunity for outstanding graduate students to live in&nbsp;a region of Latin America and work with universities, international institutions or community organizations that are improving the health and social conditions faced by low-income and vulnerable populations in Latin America. UCLA Blum Summer Scholars support the development of specific community responses being implemented in Latin American communities faced with health-threatening conditions of poverty and other social determinants of health. Both UCLA faculty members and representatives from host organizations in Latin America provide mentorships for participating students. This program is funded by both the UC Blum Federation and UCLA Blum Center.
    </p>
    <p>In 2016, Linda Shanley donated to the program in memory of her husband, John Shanley, MD. Her donations have been utilized to partially support students who will travel to Latin America to conduct mentored research under the UCLA Blum Center&rsquo;s Summer Scholars field studies program. Scholars marked with an asterisk (*) have been funded thanks to&nbsp;her generous donation.
    </p>
    <p><strong><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Learn More: UCLA Blum Summer Scholars Program Fact Sheet</font></strong>
    </p>
    <p><strong><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: small; line-height: 21px;"><a href="/blumcenter/files/view/ucla-blum-center-fact-sheets/fact-sheets-2017/factSheets2017_Summer_Scholars.pdf" title="">English</a> ~ <a href="/blumcenter/files/view/ucla-blum-center-fact-sheets/fact-sheets-2017/factSheets2017_SP_Programa_Verano.pdf" title="">Espa&ntilde;ol</a>&nbsp;~ <a href="/blumcenter/files/view/ucla-blum-center-fact-sheets/Programa_Academico_de_Verao.pdf" title="">Portugu&ecirc;s</a>&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;</strong><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span>
    </p>
    <p><strong style="font-size: large; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;"><u>2018 UCLA Blum Center Summer Scholars</u></strong>
    </p>
    <p><strong><img alt="" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2018-summer-scholars/ryan_assaf.jpg" style="float: left; width: 188px; height: 200px; margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px;" />Ryan Assaf*</strong>
    </p>
    <p><em>Campus: UC Los Angeles</em>
    </p>
    <p>Site: Lima, Peru
    </p>
    <p>Project: HIV prevention in Lima, Peru through biomedical, behavioral, social/community, and structural strategies with a focus on the use of Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP) for HIV negative individuals, increase in testing and HIV status awareness, and increase in linkage and engagement in care in HIV positive individuals. This work will be done under the instruction of and collaboration with Dr. Kelika Konda.
    </p>
    <p>Ryan Assaf is a first-year graduate student obtaining his Master&#39;s in Public Health Epidemiology with a focus on prevention of infectious diseases locally and internationally. He got his undergraduate degree at UC Irvine where he studied Public Health Sciences and did research on HIV prevention and treatment in South Africa. Post graduation, Ryan went on to work at the AIDS Services Foundation in Orange County as a PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis) navigator and HIV counselor and educator, working primarily with communities most at risk for HIV. He is currently woFjoswrking as a graduate research student for Dr. Pamina Gorbach on identifying rates of HPV among men who have sex with men and Dr. Burton Cowgill on developing an intervention to increase HPV vaccinations among UCLA undergraduates. Besides academics, Ryan enjoys playing soccer, snowboarding, backpacking, and traveling.
    </p>
    <p><strong><img alt="" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2018-summer-scholars/julia_branco.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px; float: left; width: 150px; height: 200px;" />Julia Branco</strong>
    </p>
    <p><em>Campus: UC Berkeley</em>
    </p>
    <p>Site:&nbsp;<a href="http://www.unan.edu.ni">National Autonomous&nbsp;</a><a href="http://www.unan.edu.ni/">University of Nicaragua-Leon (UNAN</a><a href="http://www.unan.edu.ni">)</a> Leon, Nicaragua
    </p>
    <p>Project: Julia will work with the Universidad Nacional Autonoma de Nicaragua (UNAN) to explore the implication of current disaster risk management policies and strategies to vulnerable urban communities.&nbsp;
    </p>
    <p>Originally from Brazil, Julia has always been interested in topics of international relations and migration, leading her to pursue a bachelors of International Studies.&nbsp;&nbsp;After graduation, she traveled to the Thai-Burma border where she taught project management and community mobilization skills to refugee youth from a Burmese minority group. She later settled in Baltimore where she served as the equity development coordinator at the Central Baltimore Partnership, developing a community plan for inclusive development. Julia is a pursuing a Masters of City Planning at UC Berkeley&#39;s School of Environmental Design focusing on building urban resilience thorough environmental and housing strategies in Latin America.&nbsp;
    </p>
    <p><strong><img alt="" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2018-summer-scholars/cameron_brandt.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px; width: 140px; height: 200px; float: left;" />Cameron Brandt*</strong>
    </p>
    <p><em>Campus: UC Los Angeles</em>
    </p>
    <p>Site: rural Nicaragua in partnership with&nbsp;<a href="http://www.unan.edu.ni">National Autonomous&nbsp;</a><a href="http://www.unan.edu.ni/">University of Nicaragua-Leon (UNAN</a><a href="http://www.unan.edu.ni">)</a> Leon, Nicaragua
    </p>
    <p>Project: Cameron&#39;s UCLA Blum Center Summer Scholar research will be focusing on the intersection of traditional medicine and western medicine as it relates to the reproductive health of women living in rural Nicaragua. Her project will be mentored by Dr. Michael Rodriguez of the UCLA Blum Center.
    </p>
    <p>Cameron Brandt is a first year Masters of Public Health student in the Community Health Sciences Department at UCLA. She graduated from Occidental College with a Bachelors in Spanish Studies. During her junior year, she studied abroad in Arica, Chile in a Public Health and Traditional Medicine program. After obtaining her Masters, Cameron hopes to pursue a Ph.D. in Public Health.
    </p>
    <p><strong><img alt="" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2018-summer-scholars/jimmy_mahady.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px; float: left; width: 161px; height: 200px;" />Jimmy Mahady</strong>
    </p>
    <p><em>Campus: UC Berkeley</em>
    </p>
    <p>Site:<em>&nbsp;</em>Instituto Nacional de Ecolog&iacute;a y Cambio Clim&aacute;tico in Mexico City, Mexico
    </p>
    <p>Project: &nbsp;The research project is a policy-analysis-focused evaluation of best practices for the promotion of increased transportation electrification to reduce atmospheric pollution and increase social mobility in Mexico&#39;s cities. The research will be conducted in conjunction with Dr. Claudia Octaviano&#39;s team at the Instituto Nacional de Ecolog&iacute;a y Cambio Clim&aacute;tico in Mexico City.
    </p>
    <p>Jimmy Mahady is a Master of Public Policy Candidate at UC Berkeley&#39;s Goldman School of Public Policy. At Berkeley, he focuses his research on developing and improving policies that shift transportation and energy away from fossil fuels while also improving the health and well-being of people and their environment. Prior to attending graduate school, Jimmy spent three years as an energy and climate policy consultant in his hometown of Seattle and was a 2012-2013 Fulbright Fellow in Uruguay, where he researched political economy of renewable energy with several federal Uruguayan ministries. Through his research this summer, he is looking forward to continuing to build strong ties between peer climate and energy regulatory and research institutions in the US and Latin America as well as getting to know Mexico City and its people.
    </p>
    <p><strong><img alt="" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2018-summer-scholars/florencia_rojo.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px; float: left; width: 133px; height: 200px;" />Florencia Rojo</strong>
    </p>
    <p><em>Campus: UC San Francisco</em>
    </p>
    <p>Site: <a href="http://cide.edu">Centro de Investigaci&oacute;n y Docencia Econ&oacute;micas (CIDE)</a> Aguascalientes, Mexico
    </p>
    <p>Project: Examining how trauma travels and the context of Central American migration to and through Mexico<em>&nbsp;</em>
    </p>
    <p>Florencia is a doctoral candidate in sociology in the Department of Social and Behavioral Sciences at UCSF.&nbsp;Her work focuses on the intersections of violence, immigration, and health. Florencia is currently working on her dissertation, which&nbsp;qualitatively&nbsp;examines violence and intergenerational trauma in the lives of Central American immigrant families. Prior to being at UCSF,&nbsp;Florencia worked with organizations in the U.S. and Latin America on community health and violence prevention/intervention initiatives.
    </p>
    <p><strong><img alt="" src="/blumcenter/files/view/2018-summer-scholars/andrew_smith.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px; float: left; width: 229px; height: 200px;" />Andrew Smith*</strong>
    </p>
    <p><em>Campus: UC Los Angeles</em>
    </p>
    <p>Site: Universidad San Francisco de Quito
    </p>
    <p>Project: Working with indigenous communities in Ecuador to ensure healthy early childhood development
    </p>
    <p>Andrew is a third-year doctoral student in the UCLA Department of Anthropology who has worked with several Shuar communities in lowland Amazonia over the past two years. Andrew&#39;s dissertation research focuses on the triadic relationship between social cognition, language, and early childhood development. Prior to joining the Department of Anthropology, Andrew received a Master&#39;s in Psychology from the UCLA Department of Psychology and a Bachelor&#39;s in Cognitive Science from Rutgers University.
    </p>
