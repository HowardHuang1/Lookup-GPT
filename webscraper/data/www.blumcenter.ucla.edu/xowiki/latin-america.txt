<p style="text-align: center; "><em style="text-align: center;"><strong style="color: rgb(23, 140, 195);"><font size="4">The UCLA Blum Center on Poverty and Health in Latin America is dedicated to the mission of bringing poverty and other social determinants of health to the forefront of research and training in Latin America.</font></strong></em></p>

<p><u><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong>Why Latin America?</strong></font></u></p>

<p><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Latin America is a region of almost 600 million diverse individuals and communities that </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">make essential&nbsp;</font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">contributions to our</font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"> </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">global society<sup>1</sup>. While the natural </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">resources, economic </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">opportunities, diversity and </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">industrious spirit of the people are </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">tremendous, persistent legacies of inequity plague </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Latin America. These challenges spread across borders and effect many Latin Americans through</font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">out the </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Americas, given widespread migration from the region. An interdisciplinary </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">approach is necessary to tackle these complex issues impacting prosperity for all Latin American communities. The mission of the UCLA Blum </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">Center on Poverty and Health in Latin America</font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">&nbsp;is to collaborate with the </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">regions institutions and communities to </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">address health challenges and pr</font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">omote solutions to issues of poverty and other social </font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">determinants of health.</font></p>

<p><u><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong>History and Demographics&nbsp;</strong></font></u></p>
</div>

<div align="left">
<div align="justify"><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">Latin Americas unique and complex history is the foundation of its present day cultural diversity</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);"> and societal structure. The region can be divided geo-politically into the following sub-regions: Mexico, the Caribbean, Ce</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">ntral America, the Guianas, Andean states, and the Southern Cone. Latin America is home to more than 400 diverse indige</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">no</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">us groups that vary linguistically, culturally, politically, and economically; their contributions, perspectives, and legacies play a vital role in the development of the </font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">region. A wealth of cultural traditions has cultivated a wide array of fine arts, film, music, da</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">nce, and lifestyles that range past the US-Mexico border down to the southern tip of South America. The region has also contributed to innovations in agriculture, math, astronomy, architecture, art, and literature that can be traced back to pre-Columbian centers of innovation and learni</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">ng such as Tenochtitlan, Chichen Itza, Machu Picchu, and Monte Alban. Nations throughout the region have harnessed its bountiful resources to drive global economic activities, which include global production and supply of products, such as coffee, sugar, bananas, and oil. The European conquest led to rapid mestizaje, or mixing of races, between European, African, Asian and indigenous populations as an influx of slaves began to settle in Latin America. This fusion of cultures transformed the language, food, beliefs and entire society of Latin America as a whole. However, the European conquest brought much political and social unrest that has driven the growth of social inequities in Latin American societies. Latin Americas historical, political, and social contexts are critical for informing effective and sustainable health interventions t</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">o address the regions health disparities. </font></font></div>
</div>

<div align="center">&nbsp;</div>

<p align="left">&nbsp;<font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);"><u>Health and Poverty in Latin America</u>&nbsp;</font></strong></font></p>


<div align="left">
<p><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">There are pressing health issues in Latin America, both new and old, that have yet to be effectively </font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">tackled. The region faces a double burden of both communicable diseases, as well as increased rates of </font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">non-communicable diseases (NCDs). Roughly 50% of child mortality in Latin Americ</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">a is </font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">due to high rates of ma</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">lnutrition, which drives four main communicable diseases: acute respiratory diseases, diarrhea, neonatal sepsis and malaria. However, there is an increasing epidemiological transition present in this region as cases of communicable diseases are being replaced with an influx of NCDs, such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancer, and chronic respiratory diseases. This influx of NCDs</font></font><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);"> is problematic throughout Latin America and is driven by high rates of obesity, hypertension, and lifestyle choices. Additionally, many other pressing challenges within the region include environmental, maternal and mental health issues. </font></font></p>

<p><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">While medical interventions are crucial in combating health challenges, they do not comprehensively address the root problems that are impacting the health of Latin Americans today. It is necessary to examine the social conditions Latinos are currently facing in order to effectively make long term, systemic and sustainable improvements in health and overall quality of life. Inequalities that exist in Latin Americas societal structures, such as gender, race and ethnicity, employment and access to education, are all essential determinants of health that need to be considered. There is an urgency to address these interconnected barriers to health in order to improve and eliminate growing disease burdens. </font></font></p>
</div>

<div align="center">&nbsp;</div>

<p align="left"><u><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">The Latin American Diaspora </font></strong></font></u></p>


<div align="left"><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);"><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">Issues of poverty and health for Latin American communities are in no way limited to the region south of the US-Mexico border. In this global era, there are various complex drivers of migration, such as civil unrest, violence, unemployment, and poverty, which have driven waves of Latin Americans to migrate across the globe, especially north to the United States. Migration of Latin Americans has extended these issues beyond Latin Americas borders, and must be addressed collaboratively via cross-border efforts. Roughly 10% of Latin Americas population has emigrated to the United States. In 2013, 17% of the United States population was composed of Latinos, making them the largest minority group<sup>2</sup>. The U.S. Census Bureau reported that in 2012, roughly 38% of Californias population was Latino and this number continues to rise<sup>3</sup>. As the population of Latin Americans continues to increase throughout the United States and other host countries, it is critical to address the health needs of these individuals while also focusing on the core health problems within Latin America itself. </font></font></font></div>

<div align="center">&nbsp;</div>

<div align="left"><u><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong><font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif"><strong><font style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">The UCLA Blum Center on Poverty and Health in Latin America </font></strong></font></strong></font></u></div>

<div align="center">&nbsp;</div>

<div align="left">
<p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">The UCLA Blum Center on Poverty and Health in Latin America aims to understand the regions social determinants of health in order to collaboratively support the development of impactful, sustainable and culturally relevant health interventions. The Center applies a comprehensive understanding of the social, economic, cultural, and political contexts throughout the Americas to tackle the complex challenges of addressing poverty and improving health for Latin American populations. The UCLA Blum Center embraces an interdisciplinary and collaborative approach that harnesses the resources and expertise of a world-class university that not only serves Latin American immigrant communities domestically, but is geographically accessible to Latin American countries. Through the creation of networks and collaborations among institutions, innovative research approaches and solutions in health policy and practice are developed and implemented throughout the Americas.</span></p>
