				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">How to Use Templates</h2><br/>
					<div class="col-1-9">

						<p>Print templates are designed to help you use UCLA&rsquo;s brand elements such as the logo, color palette and special fonts seamlessly in your work. </p>

						<p><strong>Formal templates</strong> serve for occasions when a dean or other
						VIP is involved. <strong>Informal templates</strong> work for all other cases. They can be altered somewhat to accommodate your specific needs. <strong>Word files</strong> are included in download bundles as an alternative to InDesign files where appropriate.</p>

						<p>While the amount of copy on a newsletter or invitation may vary, and the number of logos for department or unit sponsors at the bottom of a flyer can change, remember to avoid changing
						the size and color of a logo. Visit the relevant sections of this site for more information on <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/logos-and-marks">logos</a>, <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/colors">colors</a>, <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/copy-editorial">copy</a> and <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/imagery">imagery</a>.</p>

						<p>Note: Any materials involving Chancellor Block, Mrs. Block and/or Executive Vice Chancellor and Provost Carter must be approved through the Special Events and Protocol division of UCLA Strategic Communications.
						Allow seven days for the approval process. <a href="http://specialevents.ucla.edu/documents/Chancellor%20Event%20Promotional%20Materials%20Protocol%20Guide.pdf" target="_blank">Continue to the Protocol Guide for Chancellor Event Marketing Materials.</a></p>
					</div>
				</div>
