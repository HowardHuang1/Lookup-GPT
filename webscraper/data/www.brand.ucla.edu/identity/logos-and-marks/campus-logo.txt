		    	<h1 class="parent col-1-13">Campus Logo</h1>

		    	<div class="jump-nav-links col-1-13">
			    	<ul>
				    	<li><a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="#usage">Usage</a></li>

	    <section id="overview" class="element top">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <div class="col-wide col-1-9 page-overview">
				    <p class="no-bottom-margin">
				    	The core element in UCLA&rsquo;s graphic identity is the Campus Logo. It was designed by Keith Bright, a UCLA alumnus and prominent graphic designer. The inspiration for the lettering is the Bauhaus design movement — like UCLA, launched in 1919. Adopted in 2004, the logotype is simple and modern, with a slight slant to give it a dynamic feel.
				    </p>
			    </div>

		    </div>
	    </section>


	    <section id="usage" class="element">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Usage</h2>

			    <div class="col-wide col-1-7">
				   <figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos.jpg" alt="UCLA campus logo, white lettering reversed out of UCLA Blue box">
				   </figure>
			    </div>

			    <div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	The Campus Logo comes in both “boxed” and “unboxed” versions. The “boxed” logo offers impressive visual weight and puts the UCLA Blue color front and center, and is therefore the preferred version. But in some contexts, the “unboxed” version is the better design option.
			    	</p>

			    	<h3>Campus Units</h3>

			    	<p>
			    		Policy 110 reaffirms that the campus logo is the standard logo for all academic and administrative units. The campus logo is a <bold>required</bold> brand element, to be used in accordance with the guidelines on this page.
			    	</p>

			    	<p>
			    		If you need to combine the campus logo with the name of your school or department, see <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/department-logos"> Department Logos.</a>
			    	</p>

			    	<h3>Student Groups and Campus Organizations</h3>

					<p>
						The Campus Logo may not be used by student groups and other campus organizations as they cannot represent themselves as speaking on behalf of UCLA. To learn more, visit the <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/fundamentals/brand-protection"> Brand Protection </a> section. Individual students may be able to use the Campus Logo on research posters. See the <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/application/presentations">  Presentations </a> section.
					</p>
		    	</div>
		    </div>
	    </section>



	    <section id="logo-colors" class="element">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Logo Colors</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-2.jpg" alt="UCLA campus logo on white background, UCLA campus logo on black background">
						</figure>

		    	</div>

		    	<div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	The campus logo may only appear in three colors: UCLA Blue, plain white, or solid black. No gray or grayscale versions are permitted.
			    	</p>

			    	<span class="h5">The boxed logo can be:</span>

			    	<ul class="unordered-list no-margin">
				    	<li>White letters in a UCLA Blue box (preferred)</li>
				    	<li>White letters in a black box</li>
				    	<li>Black letters in a white box</li>
			    	</ul>

			    	<span class="h5">The unboxed logo can be:</span>

			    	<ul class="unordered-list no-margin">
				    	<li>UCLA Blue letters on white</li>
				    	<li>Black letters on white</li>
				    	<li>White letters on black or dark background</li>
			    	</ul>

						<p>
							Always use the logo artwork from the <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="#downloads">Downloads</a> section to assure logo colors are correct. The colors were meticulously assigned to create continuity and consistency.
						</p>
		    	</div>

		    </div>
	    </section>



	    <section id="placement" class="element">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Placement</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-3.jpg" alt="need alt text here">
							<figcaption>
								Examples of the campus logo in use
							</figcaption>
						</figure>

		    	</div>

		    	<div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	Take care in positioning the campus logo. Choose the <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="#size">minimum size</a> or larger, and provide at least the <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="#space">minimum clear space</a> around the logo. Typically booklets, brochures and reports use the logo on the front or back covers. Flyers and ads often use the logo near the bottom to anchor the layout.
			    	</p>

			    	<p>
				    	Only rarely is the campus logo the focal point of a layout. A photograph, headline or title may well be larger or carry more visual weight. However, the logo does need prominent placement and proportional size so it doesn&rsquo;t disappear from the page.
			    	</p>

			    	<p class="no-bottom-margin">
				    	Never use the logo in place of text in headlines or copy. When the UCLA name appears within a headline or body copy, simply set the letters UCLA in all caps in the same typeface and size as the surrounding text.
			    	</p>
		    	</div>

		    </div>
	    </section>



	    <section id="space" class="element">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Minimum Clear Space</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-4.jpg" alt="Clear space diagram">
						</figure>
		    	</div>

		    	<div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	Never crowd the logo. It needs clear space, &ldquo;buffer&rdquo; space, around it. Use the width of the letter &ldquo;U&rdquo; to gauge the amount of clear space that&rsquo;s
				    	needed.
			    	</p>

			    	<p>
				    	It&rsquo;s fine to align the left hand edge of the logo with your text or image: the empty space in the margin will function as the necessary clear space.
			    	</p>

			    	<p class="no-bottom-margin">
				    	The <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/department-logos">department logo</a> system allows for tighter integration of the campus logo and the administrative or academic unit name.
			    	</p>
		    	</div>

		    </div>
	    </section>



	    <section id="size" class="element">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Minimum Size</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-5.jpg" alt="minimum size diagram">
						</figure>

		    	</div>

		    	<div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	Logo size should be proportional to other elements of the layout. In no case should the logo be sized below these minimums, measured vertically.
			    	</p>

			    	<p>
				    	The unboxed version of the campus logo should be at least 0.3&rdquo; high in print, equivalent to 26 pixels high online.
			    	</p>

			    	<p class="no-bottom-margin">
				    	The boxed version of the campus logo should be at least 0.4&rdquo; high in print, equivalent to 30 pixels high online.
			    	</p>
		    	</div>

		    </div>
	    </section>



	    <section id="do-not" class="element grid last-row">
		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Do Not</h2>

				<div class="col-1-13 no-padding">

					<div class="grid-3x3">
					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-1.jpg" alt="Blue campus logo with gold drop shadow">
						    <p>Do not add a drop shadow to the logo.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-2.jpg" alt="Blue campus logo with UC larger than LA">
						    <p>Do not change the relative sizes of &ldquo;UC&rdquo; and &ldquo;LA,&rdquo; or otherwise emphasize letter pairs.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-3.jpg" alt="Gray campus logo">
						    <p>Do not use any shade of gray or grayscale for the campus logo.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-4.jpg" alt="Gold campus logo with wavy lettering">
						    <p>Do not use gold for the logo. Do not warp the lettering.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-5.jpg" alt="Blue campus logo with gold sun and white star">
						    <p>Do not add graphic elements to the campus logo.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-6.jpg" alt="White campus logo breaking out of a parallelogram">
						    <p>Do not alter the shape of the boxed logo.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-7.jpg" alt="Blue campus logo against gold">
						    <p>Do not use the UCLA Blue logo against a gold background.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-8.jpg" alt="White campus logo over a campus photo">
						    <p>Do not superimpose the white logo over a complex background. In photos, position the logo on neutral areas such as the sky.</p>
					    </div>

					    <div>
						    <img src="/images/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logos/id-campuslogos-dont-9.jpg" alt="Blue campus logo on black">
						    <p>Do not use the unboxed UCLA Blue logo on a black background.</p>
					    </div>
					</div>
					<figcaption>
						<strong>Note:</strong> All of these examples were created to illustrate a point. They are not the work of any campus unit.
					</figcaption>
				</div>
