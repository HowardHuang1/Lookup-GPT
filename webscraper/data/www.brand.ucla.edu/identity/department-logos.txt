			<section id="logo-system" class="element">
				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">Logo System</h2>

					<div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/dept-logos/id-deptlogo-1.jpg" alt="Diagram shows how the department logo system combines the boxed UCLA logo with the “Samueli School of Engineering” lettering." />
						</figure>

						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/dept-logos/id-deptlogo-2.jpg" alt="The department logo system applied to the UCLA College, UCLA Anderson School of Management, and UCLA School of Nursing" />
						</figure>

						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/dept-logos/id-deptlogo-3.jpg" alt="Four logos for the Bioengineering Department in the Samueli School" />
						</figure>
					</div>

					<div class="col-7-13">
						<p>Use of the standard department logo system is <strong>required</strong> for all academic and administrative units. Modifications are not permitted, but the logo system has a great deal of flexibility built in.</p>
						<p>The Samueli School of Engineering adopted the department logo system in spring 2018. Their logos illustrate the options provided and the flexibility of the system.</p>

						<h3>Boxed or Unboxed</h3>
						<p>The preferred version of the school logo uses the boxed UCLA logo with the name “Samueli School of Engineering.” There’s an alternate version using the unboxed UCLA logo.</p>

						<h3>Full Name or Shortened Name</h3>
						<p>In certain contexts &mdash; for instance, messages for alumni, students or faculty of the school &mdash; the “School of Engineering” wording is dropped, so the logo reads simply “UCLA Samueli.” Those audiences already know that Samueli is an engineering school.</p>

						<h3>Emphasis on School or Department</h3>
						<p>Departments have logos with two versions of their names. One version emphasizes the school name, with the department in smaller type. The other version reverses that relationship, putting the school name in small type and maximizing the department’s name. That way either the school or the department can be highlighted as needed.</p>

						<h3>Other UCLA Systems</h3>
						<p>Please note that <a href="http://identity.uclahealth.org" target="_blank">UCLA Health</a> has additional graphic hierarchies on the clinical side to convey structure accurately. UCLA Athletics uses the <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/logos-and-marks/script-logo">script logo</a>.</p>
					</div>

				</div>
			</section>

			<section id="logo-colors" class="element">
				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">Logo Colors</h2>

					<div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/dept-logos/id-deptlogo-4.jpg" alt="Eight color combinations, boxed or unboxed, against white or black backgrounds, are shown; one is not acceptable" />
					</div>

					<div class="col-7-13">
						<p>Department logos typically appear in combinations of UCLA Blue and black, as shown. All black, all white and all blue department logos are also acceptable. Never use the department logo in gray or grayscale.</p>

						<h4>The boxed version of the department logo can be:</h4>
						<ul class="unordered-list">
							<li>UCLA Blue and black on white background (preferred)</li>
							<li>UCLA Blue and white on black or dark background</li>
							<li>All black on white background</li>
							<li>All white on black or dark background</li>
						</ul>

						<h4>The unboxed version of the department logo can be:</h4>
						<ul class="unordered-list">
							<li>UCLA Blue and black on white background</li>
							<li>All black on white background</li>
							<li>All white on black or dark background</li>
						</ul>

						<p>Always use the logo artwork from the brand bundle prepared for you by UCLA Marketing. Learn how to <a class="gtm-internal-page-link" href="/identity/department-logos#request">request</a> one for your department below.</p>
					</div>

				</div>
			</section>

			<section id="minimum-space" class="element">
				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">Minimum Clear Space</h2>

					<div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/dept-logos/id-deptlogo-5.jpg" alt="Four diagrams showing width of “U” as the measure of required clear space around department logos" />
					</div>

					<div class="col-7-13">
						<p>Never crowd department logos. Like the campus logo, they need clear space, “buffer” space, to achieve visual impact. Use the width of the letter “U” to gauge the amount of clear space that’s needed.</p>
						<p>It’s fine to align the left hand edge of the logo with your text or image. The empty space in the margin will function as the necessary clear space.</p>
					</div>

				</div>
			</section>

			<section id="minimum-size" class="element">
				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">Minimum Size</h2>

					<div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/dept-logos/id-deptlogo-6.jpg" alt="Four diagrams showing minimum sizes for department logos" />
						</figure>
					</div>

					<div class="col-7-13">
						<p>Never use department logos below the minimum size, measured vertically. Note that the unboxed two-line logo with “School of Engineering” needs more vertical space than the one-line version.</p>
						<p>The one-line unboxed version should be at least 0.3” high in print, equivalent to 26 pixels high online. The two-line unboxed version should be at least 0.4” high in print, equivalent to 30 pixels high online.</p>
						<p>The boxed versions should be at least 0.4” high in print, equivalent to 30 pixels high online.</p>
					</div>

				</div>
			</section>

			<section id="other-graphics" class="element">
				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">Other Graphics</h2>

					<div class="col-1-13">
						<p>Campus units — including but not limited to centers, institutes and ORUs — who have previously used other unique logos as part of their visual identities can maintain the use of those existing graphics for a limited time while transitioning to the department <a href="/identity/department-logos#logo-system">logo system</a>. Work with your <a href="/assets/identity/MarComm-Leads-List-2.3.2023.xlsx">communications or marketing point of contact</a> to determine the timeline for that transition.</p>
						<p>A wide range of <a href="/application/print#other-graphics">print templates</a> and <a href="/application/email/templates#other-graphics">email templates</a> — stationery, event invites, newsletters and more — have been provided to guide your usage of the campus and department logos during this transition. Employ your existing graphic in the ample white space opposite the department logo available on any given template.</p>
						<p>The default “GRFK” on these templates denotes the area where your graphic should be placed to ensure it stands out, without detracting from the department logo. Neither the height nor the width of the graphic may exceed the dimensions of the boxed UCLA logo; give the audience the visual reminder that your unit lives within a larger department and UCLA.</p>
						<p>On applications where space is limited — on, say, a mug or a keychain — simply use the <a href="/identity/logos-and-marks/campus-logo">campus logo</a>.</p>
					</div>

				</div>
			</section>

				<div class="grid-wrapper">

					<h2 class="col-1-13">Request Logos</h2>

					<div class="col-wide col-1-9">
						<h3>Who Creates My Department Logos?</h3>
						<p>In addition to using a special branded version of Helvetica, school and department logos require individual fine-tuning by hand, including significant adjustments to spacing. For that reason, this site does not show the specifications for department logos. Instead, UCLA Marketing will create the digital masters for academic and administrative units.</p>

						<h3>Who Should I Contact?</h3>
						<p>For all academic and administrative units, please direct requests through your school or department’s communications or marketing office so that a consistent package of logos is created for the entire school or division at the same time.</p>
						<p>For campus programs, initiatives and partnerships (when two or more campus units — schools, departments, etc., are coordinating an event, program, etc.), please contact your unit’s Marketing & Communications Director for assistance. For partnerships with third parties (any off-campus organization, company, etc.), please submit a request at <a href="https://marks.ucla.edu" target="_blank">marks.ucla.edu</a></p>
						<p>Download a list of Marketing &amp; Communications Directors from schools and departments across campus <a href="/assets/identity/MarComm-Leads-List-2.3.2023.xlsx">here</a>. Units within UCLA Health or David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA please visit <a href="http://identity.uclahealth.org" target="_blank">identity.uclahealth.org</a>. If your campus unit is not listed, please scroll down to submit a request online.</p>

						<h3>What Will I Receive?</h3>
						<p>You will receive a brand bundle including the following variations:</p>
						<ul class="unordered-list">
							<li>Boxed and unboxed campus logo</li>
							<li>Blue, black, and white color versions</li>
							<li>Three graphic formats: EPS, SVG and PNG
								<ul class="unordered-list">
									<li>EPS for print (InDesign), engraving, banners</li>
									<li>SVG for motion graphics and animation</li>
									<li>PNG for online and Microsoft Office</li>
								</ul>
							</li>
							<li>HTML email headers</li>
						</ul>

						<p>In some cases, name variations will be created — such as “UCLA Samueli” and “UCLA Samueli School of Engineering.”</p>
