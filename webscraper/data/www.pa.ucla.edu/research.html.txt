      <h5><a name="astro">Astronomy and Astrophysics Division</a></h5><br />
      <p><img src="images/kecks.jpg" width="300px" class="image1 float-left" hspace="15px" alt="..."> Groundbreaking research, cutting-edge 
      technology, award-winning faculty – UCLA’s Division of Astronomy & Astrophysics offers a rewarding environment to pursue higher education 
      and topical research. All members of the Division carry out active research programs that garner widespread international recognition. 
      Doctoral students can participate in a variety of research projects.</p>
      <p>Research activities include the following areas.
      		<ul>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/research-area/extrasolar-planet-research-and-planetary-science">Extrasolar Planets and Planetary Science</a></li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.physics.ucla.edu/astroparticle-physics/">Astroparticle physics</a></li>
      			<li><a href="https://galacticcenter.astro.ucla.edu/">Galactic Center Group</a></li>
      		</ul>
      	</p>

      		<ul>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/becklin.html">Eric Becklin</a> focuses on the observational studies of the regions around the Massive Black Hole 
      			in the center of the Milky Way and other regions of star formation in the Galaxy. He is the Chief Scientific Advisor on SOFIA.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://astro.ucla.edu/~tdo/">Tuan Do</a> works to understand the formation of the nuclei of galaxies - He's currently studying the composition and star formation history of the Milky Way nuclear star 
      			cluster and how these stars interact with the supermassive black hole at the Galactic center.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~fitz/">Mike Fitzgerald</a> is interested in extrasolar planets and their formation using indirect and direct techniques such as adaptive optics, coronagraphy, and interferometry. 
      			He also works to develop infrared instrumentation.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~sfurlane/index.htm">Steve Furlanetto</a>  studies theoretically the first galaxies in the Universe and their effects on the Universe around them.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~ghez/">Andrea Ghez</a> is carrying out a diffraction-limited study of the Galaxy's central stellar cluster. This program has provided strong evidence for a supermassive black hole at the center 
      			of the Milky Way and is continuing to study the environs of this region.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~hansen/">Brad Hansen</a> studies the evolution of white dwarf stars and the formation and evolution of planetary systems.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/james-larkin">James Larkin</a> uses infrared spectroscopy and diffraction limited imaging to study nearby galaxies.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/matthew-malkan">Matthew Malkan</a>investigates active galactic nuclei, quasars, and the evolution of young galaxies.</li>
      			<li><a href="https://irlab.astro.ucla.edu/">Ian McLean</a> is director of the Infrared Astronomy Laboratory at UCLA and studies sub-stellar mass objects (brown dwarfs).</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~morris/">Mark Morris</a> studies the fate of massive stars and star clusters at the Galactic center, and is trying to determine the character and the origin of the magnetic field in the central few hundred 
      			parsecs of the Galaxy. He also studies the hot, diffuse gas at the Galactic center with the Chandra and XMM X-ray observatories.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/smadar-naoz">Smadar Naoz</a> is studying structure formation in the very early Universe. This involves both analytical and numerical simulations 
      			of the first gas rich and poor objects.</li>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/newman.html">William Newman</a> is a professor in the Departments of Earth, Planetary, and Space Sciences, Physics and Astronomy, and Mathematics. 
      			His astrophysical research focuses on the the dynamics and statistics of galaxy clusters, magnetic fields in the intercluster medium, 
      			and accretion processes in AGNs. His broader research relates to nonlinear dynamical problems, such as those encountered in solar system 
      			dynamics.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~rene/">Rene Ong's</a> research focuses on the astrophysics of the high-energy Universe, as revealed by gamma rays and cosmic rays, and on the quest to 
      			understand the nature of dark matter. </li>
      			<li><a href="faculty-webpages/petigura.html">Erik Petigura's</a> main research interests are the discovery and characterization of extrasolar planets, using ground- and space-based telescopes.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/michael-rich">Michael Rich</a> of the Galaxy Evolution Explorer team focuses on using GALEX to address star formation in the universe.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/shoko-sakai">Shoko Sakai</a>  investigates star formation rate and 3D structure of the local universe.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/alice-shapley">Alice Shapley</a>  explores optical/infrared imaging and spectroscopic observations of high-redshift galaxies.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/personnel/jean-turner">Jean Turner</a>  studies star formation in 
      			starbursts, especially super star cluster formation.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~vvv/">Vladimir Vassiliev</a> uses ground-based gamma-ray astronomy with VERITAS and Whipple to study Extragalactic Background Light and high energy gamma ray sources.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~wright/intro.html">Edward (Ned) Wright</a> is interested in infrared astronomy and cosmology. He is the PI on the Wide-field Infrared Survey Explorer (WISE).</li>
      		</ul>

      		<h5><a name="amo">Atomic-Molecular-Optical (AMO) Physics</a></h5><br />
      		<p><img src="images/amo.jpg" width="300px" class="image1 float-left" hspace="15px" alt="..."> The UCLA AMO group 
      		develops methods to bring new forms of quantum matter under control and uses existing systems to 
      		demonstrate new technologies and measurements. Goals include understanding quantum magnetism using 
      		quantum information protocols in trapped ions, and developing new methods for producing ultracold molecules and molecular ions.</p>
      		<p>Research activities include the following areas.
      		<ul>
      			<li><a href="https://campbellgroup.physics.ucla.edu/">Campbell Group</a></li>
      			<li><a href="http://hamiltonlab.physics.ucla.edu/">Hamilton Group</a></li>
      			<li><a href="http://hudsongroup.physics.ucla.edu/">Hudson Group</a></li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.cqse.ucla.edu/">Center for Quantum Science and Engineering</a></li>
      		</ul>
      		</p>

      	<h5><a name="hard">Hard Condensed Matter</a></h5><br />
      	<p><img src="images/hard_condensed.jpg" width="300px" class="image1 float-left" hspace="15px" alt="..."> This Physics & Astronomy faculty group is active in the 
      	discovery of novel electronic materials, correlated electron 
      	systems, unconventional superconductivity, quantum magnetism, heavy-fermion physics, quantum critical phenomena, semiconductor physics, spintronics, 
      	quantum statistical mechanics and field theory, topological states of matter, and Majorana fermions. </p>
      	<p>Research activities include the following areas.
      	
      	</p>
      	
      	<div id="accordion" id="accordionExample">
  		<div class="card" style="width: 45rem;">
    	<div class="card-header">
      	<a class="card-link" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseThree">
        Click here to view details of the Hard Condensed Matter research</a>
    	</div>
    	<div id="collapseThree" class="collapse" data-parent="#accordion">
      	<div class="card-body">
        	<p></p>
      		<ul>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/brown.html">Stuart Brown</a> investigates the roles of dimensionality, electronic correlations, and disorder in the properties of electronic materials. Most of 
      			the systems we study are quasi-one dimensional or quasi-two dimensional crystals, and in some cases powders.</li>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/chakravarty.html">Sudip Chakravarty's</a> research interest involves quantum theory of collective behavior of electronic systems. He is interested in theories of 
      			high temperature superconductivity, dissipative quantum systems, quantum phase transition and criticality, localization transition in interacting 
      			systems, and the concept of von Neumann entropy in quantum phase transitions.</li>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/gutierrez.html">Christopher Gutiérrez</a> investigates the quantum properties of low-dimensional materials using atomically-resolved scanning probe and momentum-resolved photoemission 
      			experiments.</li>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/holczer.html">Karoly Holczer</a> studies experimental condensed matter physics, organic conductors and superconductors, and electron paramagnetic resonance.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://www.physics.ucla.edu/~jiangh/">HongWen Jiang</a> focuses on experiments on the interactions between electrons and magnetic fields in various quantum Hall systems and electron 
      			localization phenomena.</li>
      			<li><a href="faculty-websites/kogar.html">Anshul Kogar</a> studies highly nonlinear effects with ultrafast probes of materials. </li>
      			<li><a href="http://nilab.physics.ucla.edu/">Ni Ni</a> focuses on the characterization of physical properties and structures of materials through thermodynamic, transport, X-ray and neutron 
      			measurements, with an emphasis on the design, synthesis, and crystal growth of new materials.</li>
      			<li><a href="http://regangroup.physics.ucla.edu/">B. Chris Regan's</a> long term goals include a better understanding of the overlap between thermodynamics and quantum mechanics, and the construction of 
      			a model system for the investigation of clean energy harvesting. Areas of expertise include carbon nanotubes, graphene, nanofabrication and in situ 
      			transmission electron microscopy. </li>
      			<li><a href="http://cmt-roy.physics.ucla.edu/">Rahul Roy's</a> work is in the field of condensed matter theory, which is devoted to the study of systems with many particles where quantum effects 
      			are important. A particular theme of his research is the study of topological phases and other topological aspects of condensed matter. </li>
            <li><a href=" " >Qianhui Shi</a> studies quantum phases of matter built from 2D van der Waals materials.  Main research approaches include the designing and fabrication of novel device structures, quantum transport and capacitance measurements at low temperatures and high magnetic fields. </li>
      			<li><a href="https://sites.google.com/site/tserkovnyak/">Yaroslav Tserovnyak</a> studies quantum transport and nonequilibrium dynamics in 
      			low-dimensional electron systems, with a focus on spin and topology.</li>
      		</ul>

      	<h5><a name="eep">Experimental Elementary Particle Physics</a></h5><br />
      	<p><img src="images/cyclotron-vintage.jpg" width="300px" class="image1 float-left" hspace="15px" alt="...">EEP is involved in experimental and theoretical research in the areas of dark matter, high-energy astrophysics (using 
      	gamma rays, cosmic rays and neutrinos), cosmology, and physics at the Large Hadron Collider. </p>
      	<br /><br /><br />
      	<p>Research activities include the following areas.</p>

  		<h5><a name="tep">Theoretical Elementary Particles</a></h5><br />

      	several postdocs and students each year. </p>

  <h5><a name="tepapp">Theory of Elementary Particles, Astroparticle Physics, and Phenomenology</a></h5><br />

  <h5><a name="nuclear">Nuclear Physics</a></h5><br />
      	<p><img src="images/nuclear.jpg" width="300px" class="image1 float-left" hspace="15px" alt="...">The nuclear physics research is 
      	focused on understanding the matter composed of quarks and gluons as described by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), which makes up 
      	99% of the visible mass of the universe; as well as the fundamental nature of the neutrinos. Current research includes both 
      	experimental nuclear physics and theoretical nuclear physics.</p>
      	<p><p>Research activities include the following areas.
      		<ul>
      			<li><a href="http://nuclear.physics.ucla.edu/">Nuclear Physics Group</a></li>

  <h5><a name="plasma">Plasma and Advanced Accelerators</a></h5><br />
