	The Day the Infant Internet Uttered its First Words</h1>
<p>
	Below is a record of the first message ever sent over the ARPANET. It took place at 22:30 hours on October 29, 1969. This record is an excerpt from the &quot;IMP Log&quot; that was kept at UCLA. Professor Kleinrock was supervising his student/programmer Charley Kline (CSK) and they set up a message transmission to go from the UCLA SDS Sigma 7 Host computer to another programmer, Bill Duvall, at the SRI SDS 940 Host computer. The transmission itself was simply to &quot;login&quot; to SRI from UCLA. They succeeded in transmitting the &quot;l&quot; and the &quot;o&quot; and then the system crashed! Hence, the first message on the Internet was <strong>&quot;lo&quot;, </strong>as in &quot;lo and behold!&nbsp;They were able to do the full login about an hour later.</p>
