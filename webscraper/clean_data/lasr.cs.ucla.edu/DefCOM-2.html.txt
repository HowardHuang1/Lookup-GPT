<div style="margin-left: 40px;"><span style="font-weight: bold;"><span
 style="font-style: italic;">DefCOM&nbsp;</span> is a three-year
NSF-funded collaborative research grant with the Professor Jelena
Mirkovic and the University of Delaware.&nbsp; Under this grant, the <a
 href="http://lasr.cs.ucla.edu">LASR</a>
lab shall demonstrate that an effective defense against flooding
distributed denial-of-servicer (DDoS) attacks, and collatoral damage
can be achieved practically through a sparse deploymet of an overlay
network of defense nodes.<br>
<br>
Many critical funstions today are realized through the use of network
services, and DDoS attacks represent a major threat to network
operations.&nbsp; They overwhelm a key resource at the victim network,
generating a flood of seemingly legitimate packets, deny services to
legitimate clients and frequently create heavy Internet
congestion.&nbsp; Attackers need not possess any particular skill to
perpetrate DDoS attacks, and face virtually no risk of
attribution.&nbsp; In spite of numerous research and commercial efforts,
there are still no effective DDoS defenses.<br>
<br>
<span style="font-style: italic;">DefCOM</span>, our distributed DDoS
defense system, will distribute nodes throughout the Internet
(organized into peer-to-peer overlays) and will act jointly to detect
and respond to DDoS attacks.&nbsp; Attack resonse is twofold:&nbsp; on
one hand, defense nodes constrain susicious traffic, relieving the
victim from high-volume incoming streams that consume resources; on the
other hand, nodes cooperate to detect legitimate traffic within the
suspicious streams and ensure its correct delivery.&nbsp; Thus, <span
 style="font-style: italic;">DefCOM</span> achieves the primary goal of
DDoS defense -- that of cancelling the denial-of-service effect.&nbsp;
Additionally, the system has a solid economic model where networks
deploying defense nodes directly benefit from their operation.&nbsp; <span
 style="font-style: italic;">DefCOM</span> further offers a framework
for existing security systems to join the overlay and cooperate in the
defense.&nbsp; These features create an excellent motivation for wide
deployment and the possibility of a large impact on the DDoS threat.<br
