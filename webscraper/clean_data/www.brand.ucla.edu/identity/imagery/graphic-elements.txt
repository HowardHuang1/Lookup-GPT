	<section id="molecule" class="element top">
		<div class="grid-wrapper">

			<h2 class="col-1-13">The Molecule</h2>
			<p class="col-1-13">The molecule is a unique design element used throughout the UCLA brand. Inspired by California sunshine and natural sunspots captured in photography, the UCLA molecule represents light and energy as well as a spirit of scientific inquiry and discovery. Molecules are most effective when used sparingly.</p>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/molecules-lens-flare.jpg" alt="UCLA Molecules arranged as a lens flare" />
				</figure>
			</div>
			<div class="col-7-13">
				<h3>Molecules as lens flares</h3>
				<p>Transparent molecules can be used to form lens flares. Make sure to arrange molecules in one line across the image, not grouped heavily in clusters.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/molecules-headline.jpg" alt="UCLA Molecule with headline" />
				</figure>
			</div>
			<div class="col-7-13">
				<h3>Molecules containing headlines</h3>
				<p>A large molecule may be used to hold a headline and short sub-headline. Set the type in a contrasting color to ensure legibility. This execution is effective in print pieces only.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/id-imagery-graphic-4.jpg" alt="UCLA Molecule as container" />
				</figure>
			</div>
			<div class="col-7-13">
				<h3>Molecules containing factoids or quotes</h3>
				<p>A large molecule may be used to hold factoids and other small bits of information. An outlined molecule using a 1-point rule may be used on top of or interlaced with a solid molecule to add dimension. Again, be sure to use molecules in this way on print projects only.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/molecules-graphic-device.jpg" alt="UCLA molecules as small graphic devices" />
				</figure>
			</div>
			<div class="col-7-13">
				<h3>Molecules as small graphic devices</h3>
				<p>Three molecules can be used as small graphic devices or accents for headlines.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/imagery/id-imagery-graphic-7.jpg" alt="UCLA molecules as overlapping elements" />
				</figure>
			</div>
			<div class="col-7-13">
				<h3>Molecules as overlapping elements</h3>
				<p class="no-bottom-margin">A molecule may be used as an overlapping background element to draw the readerâ€™s eye to a headline or section heading.</p>
			</div>

		</div>
	</section>


	<section id="overlay" class="element">
		<div class="grid-wrapper">

			<h2 class="col-1-13">The Overlay Box</h2>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/overlay.jpg" alt="Overlay box" />
				</figure>
			</div>

			<div class="col-7-13">
				<p class="no-bottom-margin">Another branding element in the UCLA design identity is the overlay box. This gives shape and form to our brand identity. Applied consistently, it can provide instant recognition to your work.</p>
			</div>

		</div>
	</section>


	<section id="ribbon" class="element">
		<div class="grid-wrapper">

			<h2 class="col-1-13">The Ribbon Treatment</h2>

			<div class="col-1-7">
				<figure>
					<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/ribbon.jpg" alt="Ribbon treatment" />
				</figure>
			</div>

			<div class="col-7-13">
				<p class="no-bottom-margin">Ribbon treatments bring emphasis to headlines. When using ribbon treatments over photos, try not to be too wordy. Short, punchy sentences are best because they do not distract from the image.</p>
			</div>

		</div>
	</section>


	<section id="do-not" class="element grid">
		<div class="grid-wrapper">

			<h2 class="col-1-13">Do Not</h2>

			<div class="col-1-13 no-padding">

				<div class="grid-3x3">
					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-1.jpg" alt="A block of copy within a molecule" />
						<p>Do not set blocks of copy within a molecule.</p>
					</div>

					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-2.jpg" alt="Justified copy within a molecule" />
						<p>Do not justify copy within a molecule.</p>
					</div>

					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-3.jpg" alt="A darkened molecule" />
						<p>Do not make the molecule too dark.</p>
					</div>

					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-4.jpg" alt="A distorted molecule" />
						<p>Do not distort the molecule.</p>
					</div>

					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-5.jpg" alt="A molecule at 100% opacity" />
						<p>Do not use molecules at 100% opacity.</p>
					</div>

					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-7.jpg" alt="Six molecules in a row used as a headline accent" />
						<p>Do not use more than three molecules at a time for headline accents.</p>
					</div>

					<div>
						<img src="/images/identity/graphic-elements/graphic-dont-6.jpg" alt="An image of the Bruin statue within a molecule" />
						<p>Do not put an image inside a molecule.</p>
					</div>

				</div>
			</div>

		</div>
	</section>
