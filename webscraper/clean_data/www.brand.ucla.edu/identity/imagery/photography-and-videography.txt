			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Art Direction</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/imagery/photo-and-videography/id-imagery-photovideo-2.jpg" alt="UCLA undergrads walking on campus">

		    	<div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	Imagery should capture UCLA&rsquo;s unique personality, conveying warmth, energy and the feeling that anything is possible. To that end, photos and videos should feel bright and inspirational, with light playing a central role. The sky and the sun represent the brand&rsquo;s themes as well as the characteristic weather, light and location of campus. Natural light is essential, and plentiful sunshine, natural washouts and sun flares are welcome.

		    <div class="grid-wrapper">

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Campus and Los Angeles</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-7">
						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/imagery/photo-and-videography/id-imagery-photovideo-4.jpg" alt="UCLA undergrads walking on campus">
						</figure>

						<figure>
							<img src="/images/identity/imagery/photo-and-videography/id-imagery-photovideo-5.jpg" alt="A view of the front of Saxon Suites">
						</figure>
		    	</div>

		    	<div class="col-7-13">
			    	<p>
				    	The sky plays a key role in UCLA&rsquo;s imagery &mdash; it conveys a feeling of possibility and opportunity. When taking photos of campus and Los Angeles, keep as much sky present in the photo as possible. Photos may also be taken from directly below to create dramatic crops of campus and Los Angeles, but this should be used sparingly. Nighttime imagery should be kept to a minimum, but when necessary, lights (city lights, street lights, etc.) should be prominent.
			    	</p>

		    	</div>

		    </div>

			    <h2 class="col-1-13">Photo and Video Specs</h2>

			    <div class="col-1-9">

				    <h3>Alt Text</h3>
						<p>
				    	Image alt text (alternative text) is used within HTML code to describe the appearance and function of an image on a web page. Alt text is a central principle of web accessibility. Visually impaired people using screen readers read alt text to better understand the image, and it also provides context to search engines, helping them to index an image properly. When writing alt text, describe the image as specifically as possible, but keep it short &mdash; the most popular screen readers cut off alt text at around 125 characters.
				    </p>
					<h3>File Sizes and Specs</h3>

					<dl>
			    	<dt><strong>Print images:</strong></dt> <dd>CMYK color format, TIFF, PSD, or JPEG at 300 dpi</dd>
			    	<dt><strong>Web/digital images:</strong></dt> <dd>RGB Color format, JPEG or PNG ranging from 72 dpi to 300 dpi</dd>
		    	</dl>

			    </div>
		    </div>
	    </section>
