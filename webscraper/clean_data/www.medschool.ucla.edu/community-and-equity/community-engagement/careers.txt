<p>Administrative Clinical Care Partner provides care and assistance to patients with limited physical abilities, which they are unable to complete on their own, such as walking and bathing. Administrative Clinical Care Partners often report to registered nurses (RNs) or licensed practical or licensed vocational nurses (LVNs).</p>

    <div class="prose">
      <h3>What they do</h3>

<p>Surgical technologists assist in surgical operations under the supervision of surgeons, registered nurses or other surgical personnel. ey are members of operating room teams, which most commonly include surgeons, anesthesiologists and circulating nurses.</p>

<p>Surgical techs can work in a variety of locations, including hospitals, physicians', dentists' offices, outpatient clinics or military hospitals.</p>

<p>The main job duties are:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Shaving and sterilization of patients</li>
	<li>Sterilization of operating rooms and all equipment</li>
	<li>Monitoring patients' vital statistics</li>
	<li>Making sure all equipment is functioning</li>
	<li>Handing the surgeon tools during procedures</li>
	<li>Stocking and restocking operating rooms before and after surgeries</li>
</ul>

<h3>Education needed</h3>

<p>Associate's Degree or 12-month Certificate Program</p>

      <h3>What they do</h3>

<p>An ultrasound technician, often called a diagnostic medical sonographer or simply a sonographer, operates special equipment that uses sound waves to help diagnose patients' ailments. They work directly with patients and physicians. Ultrasound technicians may specialize in obstetric and gynecologic sonography, abdominal sonography, neurosonography, breast sonography, vascular sonography or cardiac sonography. They can work in a hospital or a community clinic.</p>
