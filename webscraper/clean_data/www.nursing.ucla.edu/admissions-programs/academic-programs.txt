<div class="text prose" >
  <h2><strong>Bachelor of Science&nbsp;(this includes freshman and transfer)</strong></h2>

<p>The BS Prelicensure program is designed to prepare students for a career in professional nursing practice. This is a traditional, four-year program beginning in the freshman year. A limited number of transfer students are accepted annually. Graduates of the BS program are eligible to take the National Council Licensing Examination (NCLEX) to be&nbsp;licensed as a registered nurse (R.N.) and may apply for the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.rn.ca.gov/pdfs/applicants/phn-app.pdf">Public Health Nursing Certificate</a>&nbsp;from the California Board of Registered Nursing. The BS program is a UCLA-certified capstone major. During the last clinical course in the fourth year, each student plans and implements a clinically-based Scholarly Project designed to improve the system of care provided to patients and their families.</p>

<p>Students successfully completing the BS degree also acquire an educational foundation for entry into the master's program that prepares nurses for advanced practice roles such as nurse practitioners, clinical nurse specialists and administrators in primary and acute care.</p>

<p><a class="button button--primary" data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="db1f0515-fba6-46c0-aa1a-665f4d072131" href="/programs-admissions/academic-programs/bachelor-of-science-0" title="Bachelor of Science"><strong>View Requirements</strong></a></p>

<hr>
<h2><strong>Master of Science in Nursing - Master's Entry Clinical Nurse (MECN</strong>)</h2>

<p>The UCLA School of Nursing has an option within the Master of Science in Nursing degree program that is designed to prepare individuals with a baccalaureate degree in another discipline for a career in nursing. This two-year pre-licensure program includes summer enrollment between the first and second years.&nbsp; Those who complete the program are granted the Master in Science in Nursing (MSN) degree and are eligible to take the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ncsbn.org/about.htm">National Council Licensing Examination</a>(NCLEX) to be&nbsp;licensed as&nbsp;<a href="http://www.rn.ca.gov/careers/howto.shtml">registered nurses</a>&nbsp;(RN). They are then prepared to practice nursing at the bedside in a hospital setting.</p>

<p>Graduates of the program may also apply for a&nbsp;<a href="http://www.rn.ca.gov/pdfs/applicants/phn-app.pdf">Public Health Nursing Certificate</a>&nbsp;from the California Board of Registered Nursing.</p>

<p><a class="button button--primary" data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="b7c2362e-62f5-4030-b908-7e566088a641" href="/programs-admissions/academic-programs/master-of-science-entry" title="Master of Science - Entry"><strong>View Requirements</strong></a></p>

<hr>
<h2><strong>Master of Science in Nursing - Advanced Practice Registered Nurse (APRN</strong>)</h2>

<p>The School of Nursing offers a two-year course of graduate study leading to the Master of Science in Nursing (MSN) designed to prepare registered nurses with a bachelor's degree in nursing for advanced practice. The MSN Advanced Practice Registered Nurse/Post-licensure program prepares nurse practitioners and clinical nurse specialists for the national certification examinations in the specialty that they are admitted into.</p>

<p>Advanced practice specialties offered through the UCLA School of Nursing include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Adult/Gerontology Primary Care Population</li>
	<li>Adult/Gerontology Acute Care Population</li>
	<li>Family&nbsp;Population</li>
	<li>Pediatric Population</li>
</ul>

<p><a class="button button--primary" data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="7cf3e472-64b4-4f49-b1f7-25b380a266ad" href="/programs-admissions/academic-programs/master-of-science-advanced-practice" title="Master of Science - Advanced Practice"><strong>View Requirements</strong></a></p>

<hr>
<h2><strong>Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP)</strong></h2>

<p>A DNP is a doctorally prepared, advanced practice nurse and a clinical expert and leader on the translation of evidence-base practice to improve health outcomes on a systems level. The DNP degree program is a clinical practice program for working professional nurses. You will integrate competencies for&nbsp;advanced nursing practice roles in clinical practice, leadership, health policy and education, as well as further develop your scholarship capacity in evidence-based practice.&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Highlights:</strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>Translate research into clinical practice</li>
	<li>Elevate your executive leadership skills</li>
	<li>Influence patient outcomes and population health</li>
	<li>Earn your doctorate in less than<strong>&nbsp;two</strong><strong>&nbsp;years</strong></li>
	<li>Continue working full-time throughout the&nbsp;program</li>
	<li>Clinical practicum and&nbsp;<a href="https://nursing.ucla.edu/node/296/">DNP Scholarly Project</a>&nbsp;can be performed at your workplace</li>
</ul>

<p><a class="button button--primary" data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="4a0b1a23-e99e-4e29-9c60-976a7ce71923" href="/programs-admissions/academic-programs/doctor-of-nursing-practice" title="Doctor of Nursing Practice"><strong>View Requirements</strong></a></p>

<hr>
<h2><strong>Doctor of Philosophy (PhD)</strong></h2>

<p>The UCLA School of Nursing (SON) offers a Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree program with the goal in developing the foundation of knowledge upon which the practice of the profession is based. The UCLA SON PhD program aims to develop nurse scientists who can conduct research and generate theory that incorporate the influence of the biologic, psychosocial and physical environments on health and healthcare.&nbsp;Areas of focus and interest include, but are not limited to: health of diverse and vulnerable populations, older adults and persons with chronic and communicable diseases.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Doctoral graduates serve as leaders who educate, influence practice, advance science, optimize healthcare delivery and influence healthcare policy worldwide.</p>

<p><a class="button button--primary" data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="604529d2-e405-49ea-a5d2-72c6f13fa805" href="/programs-admissions/academic-programs/doctor-of-philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy"><strong>View Requirements</strong></a></p>

<hr>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h2><strong>UC Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner Certificate Program</strong></h2>

<p>The UC Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner Certificate Program curriculum is competency-based and aligned with the National Organization for Nurse Practitioner Faculties (NONPF) core and population specific competencies which include: scientific foundations, leadership, quality, practice inquiry, technology and information literacy, policy, health delivery system and ethics. The curriculum is built upon a framework which supports trainee knowledge, skills and attitude for development of the PMHNP role. This is supported through didactic content and a workplace-based clinical internship. The curricular framework and descriptions are:</p>

<ul>
	<li>PMHNP role development: To build excellence in practice as a PMHNP</li>
	<li>Interprofessional collaboration: To support communication and collaboration across professions</li>
	<li>Structural competency: To create healthcare professionals who understand the effect of social inequity, racism and limited access to mental healthcare on individuals and communities</li>
	<li>Self-reflective practice:&nbsp;To gain awareness of self, others and situation in the context of professional identity and role development</li>
</ul>

<p><a class="button button--primary" href="https://nursing.ucla.edu/programs-admissions/academic-programs/post-masters-certificate-psychiatric-mental-health-nurse"><strong>View Requirements</strong></a></p>
