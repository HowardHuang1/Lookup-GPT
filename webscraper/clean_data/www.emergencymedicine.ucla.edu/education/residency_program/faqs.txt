<h1 class="display-4 title"><strong>01.&nbsp;</strong>What are <strong>shifts</strong> like?</h1>

<hr>&nbsp;<img alt="--" class="responsivemin align-center" data-entity-type="file" data-entity-uuid="cf5ea720-3ad2-4729-bd1c-602d29fcb4ea" src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/shifts_0.png" width="1170" height="586" loading="lazy">
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<div class="row">
<div class="column">
<h3>— Ronald Reagan Medical Center</h3>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Mix 8/9/12hr shifts</strong>

	<ul>
		<li>12hr weekend shifts</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Main ED:</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Sr (R3/R4) + Jr (R2) + Interns</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Fast Track:</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>R2/R3/R4</li>
		<li>8hr Fast Track Shifts</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li>Scribes for seniors and in Fast Track Dictation Software Available</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="column">
<h3>— Olive View Medical Center</h3>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Mix 8/9/12hr shifts</strong>

	<ul>
		<li>12hr weekend shifts</li>
		<li>8hr weekday overnights</li>
		<li>Combined<strong> Main/Fast Track ED</strong></li>
	</ul>
	</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<ul>
	<li>Most residents stay 30 minutes after shift to wrap up</li>
	<li>Majority of shifts overlap to allow wrap up and chart completion</li>
</ul>

<h1>Trauma</h1>

<hr>
<div class="row">
<div class="column2">
<h3><strong>— Ronald Reagan</strong></h3>

<ul>
	<li>Level I&nbsp;Trauma Center</li>
	<li>EM senior is team leader</li>
	<li>Procedures alternate by day of work between EM and Trauma</li>
	<li>Airway always belongs to EM</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="column2">
<h3><strong>— Olive View</strong></h3>

<ul>
	<li>Not designated Trauma Center</li>
	<li>Still gets trauma walk-ins/drop-offs!</li>
	<li>EM residents in charge of resuscitation</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="column2">
<h3><strong>— Antelope Valley</strong></h3>

<ul>
	<li>Level II Trauma Center</li>
	<li>EM resident is only trainee present and participates in all procedures</li>
</ul>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h1 class="display-4 title"><strong>06.&nbsp;</strong>How do residents receive <strong>mentorship</strong>?</h1>

<hr>
<ul>
	<li>Each class has an Assistant/Associate Program Director who is assigned to them for all 4 years and meets with each resident formally on a semi-annual basis to track progress and discuss goals</li>
	<li>Residents are matched with personal faculty mentor to meet with regularly to discuss career or personal issues</li>
	<li>Sibling families are made up of 4 residents (one per class) to provide peer mentorship and advice</li>
	<li>The program hosts a Career Day each year for graduating R4s to give advice on how to prepare for the job search and life after residency. Many of our program's alumni from various practice types around the region attend and give their perspectives on the difference settings and locations in which EM is practiced.</li>
</ul>

<h1 class="display-4 title"><strong>08.&nbsp;</strong>What type of <strong>activities</strong> are residents involved in?</h1>

<hr>
<h2>Committees</h2>

<p>Residency committees are run entirely by the residents based on individual interests. Current committees include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Recruitment</li>
	<li>Social media</li>
	<li>Wellness</li>
	<li>Community outreach</li>
	<li>Medical education</li>
	<li>Hospital-wide committees that residents can get involved in include ethics and GME committees</li>
</ul>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h2>Organized Medicine</h2>

<p>Residents are involved in and members of ACEP, CalACEP, EMRA, AAEM/RSA, California Medical Association, AMA, and local advocacy groups such as SCOPHE.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h2>Extracurricular Medicine Activities</h2>

<p>Several of our residents have been involved in Event Medicine activities including Rock Medicine (music festival), AIDS Lifestyle support, and Painted Turtle Camp</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h1 class="display-4 title"><strong>09.&nbsp;</strong>How does the residency address <strong>diversity</strong>?</h1>

<hr>
<p>The UCLA Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA has an office of Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion (EDI) with a robust amount of programming for students, trainees, faculty, and staff. Please refer to <a href="https://medschool.ucla.edu/diversityaffairs">https://medschool.ucla.edu/diversityaffairs</a> to see a full listing of information and events!</p>

<p>In 2019 the Department of Emergency Medicine Residency Program chartered an EDI Committee. The mission of the EDI committee is to foster diversity and inclusion for the recruitment and retention of our residents, fellows, and faculty to reflect the rich diversity of Los Angeles and promote long term population health equity. Please refer to the subheading "Diversity" on our website for more information!</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h1 class="display-4 title"><strong>10.&nbsp;</strong>How does the residency address <strong>wellness</strong>?</h1>

<hr>
<p>Residency Wellness Committee plans and organizes near-monthly residency wide events that will now also include nursing/ancillary staff and scribes! Some previous activities include hiking, magic shows, and comedy shows.</p>

<p>The residency hosts 2 retreats each year and allow residents to request special days off in order to help them be present at important events in their lives outside of residency.</p>

<p>UCLA Behavioral Health Center is available for residents to seek help and support with 24/7 services provided including psychiatric consultation and short-term psychotherapy</p>

<p>UCLA Physician and Faculty Wellness Program provides lectures and workshops related to burnout, stress, health, and wellness as well as a drop-in center.</p>

<p>Residents are able to get discounted rates to join UCLA gyms and fitness centers.</p>
