Ph.D. Programs
Many of the graduate training programs in biomedical sciences at UCLA are organized under an umbrella program,
Graduate Programs in Bioscience (GPB)
. Students who are interested in pursuing doctoral studies with Integrative Biology & Physiology faculty apply to one of 11 Ph.D.-granting “Home Areas” listed under GBP. For information about the Graduate Programs in Bioscience, admissions, and the application process go to:
https://bioscience.ucla.edu/application-procedure
All faculty of the Integrative Biology & Physiology Department are members of the Molecular, Cellular & Integrative Physiology (MCIP) Ph.D. program.  For information about the MCIP Ph.D. program, admissions, and the application process go to:
https://www.mcip.ucla.edu/
Additional GPB Home Areas to which students interested in
Integrative Biology and Physiology may enroll include:
Neuroscience (NSIDP)
Bioinformatics (BIOINFO)
Cell & Developmental Biology (CDB)
Immunity, Microbes, and Molecular Pathogenesis (IMMP)
Gene Regulation Epigenomics and Transcriptomics (GREAT)
For information about the Graduate Programs in Bioscience, admissions, and the application process go to:
https://bioscience.ucla.edu/application-procedure
UCLA offers a
Medical Scientist Training Program (MSTP)
for students who aim to earn an MD/PhD under a single training program. For information about MSTP, admissions, and the application process go to:
https://mstp.healthsciences.ucla.edu/
M.D.s who want to earn a Ph.D. can apply to the
Specialty Training & Advanced Research (STAR)
program or the
Eye Specialty Training & Advanced Research (EyeSTAR)
program. For information about the two programs, admissions, and the application process go to:
STAR program, Department of Medicine:
https://medschool.ucla.edu/star
EyeSTAR program, Department of Ophthalmology:
https://www.uclahealth.org/eye/eyestar-program-1300
