Campus Resources
CAREER CENTER
: STRATHMORE BUILDING, 501 WESTWOOD PLAZA, (310) 825-2981
Provides career planning, pre-professional advising and employment assistance to current UCLA students. Most services are free; some are fee-based. (
www.career.ucla.edu
)
FINANCIAL AID
: A-129J MURPHY HALL, (310) 206-0400
Provides financial aid counseling and information to students who apply and are qualified to receive need-based aid, including grants, loans, work study, etc. (
https://www.financialaid.ucla.edu/
)
REGISTRAR’S OFFICE
: 1113 MURPHY HALL, (310) 825-1091
Handles registration, enrollment, degree services; provides official transcripts; provides verification of enrollment for insurance purposes, etc. (
www.registrar.ucla.edu
)
SCHOLARSHIP RESOURCE CENTER
: 233 COVEL COMMONS, (310) 206-2875
Provides help for students in search of private scholarships, regardless of financial aid eligibility. (
https://www.scholarshipcenter.ucla.edu
)
UCLA COUNSELING AND PSYCHOLOGICAL SERVICES (CAPS)
: JOHN WOODEN CENTER WEST, (310) 825- 0768
Provides confidential, one-on-one therapy services and other programs designed to promote the emotional and mental well-being to the UCLA community, including the Wellness Skills Programs and Workshops across campus and the Wellness Self-Help Lab at John Wooden Center West. 24-hour Help Access Line available. (
www.counseling.ucla.edu
)
UCLA CONSULTATION & RESPONSE
:
Case Managers are available to receive reports of concern and help determine an appropriate response to assist the student and those who may also be impacted by a situation. (
https://www.studentincrisis.ucla.edu/
)
ARTHUR ASHE STUDENT HEALTH & WELLNESS CENTER
: 221 WESTWOOD PLAZA, (310) 825-4073
Provides outpatient services for UCLA students; most services are prepaid by registration fees. A current Bruin Card is required for service. (
www.studenthealth.ucla.edu
)
DASHEW CENTER FOR INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS & SCHOLARS
: 106 BRADLEY HALL, (310) 825-1681
Assists international students with questions about immigration, employment, government regulations, visas, financial aid, academic and administrative procedures, cultural adjustment and personal matters (
https://www.internationalcenter.ucla.edu/
)
OFFICE OF THE DEAN OF STUDENTS
: 1206 MURPHY HALL, (310) 825-3871
Concerned with matters of disciplinary action including academic integrity (cheating, plagarism); student conduct; sexual harassment; UC policies regarding campus activities, organizations and students; Dean’s Certification; among others.
(https://www.deanofstudents.ucla.edu/)
OFFICE OF OMBUDS SERVICES
: STRATHMORE BUILDING, 501 WESTWOOD PLAZA, (310) 825-7627
Acting impartially, Ombuds office responds to issues and concerns from students, staff, faculty and administrators by investigating unresolved conflicts or facilitating resolutions
(http://www.ombuds.ucla.edu/)
CENTER FOR ACCESSIBLE EDUCATION
A-255 MURPHY HALL, (310) 825-1501
Provides academic support services to regularly enrolled students with documented permanent or temporary disabilities in compliance with Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990 and University policies. (
www.cae.ucla.edu
)
STUDENT LEGAL SERVICES
: 70 DODD HALL, (310) 825-9894
Provides legal assistance from attorneys and/or law students (under direct supervision by attorneys) to currently registered and enrolled students. (https://www.studentlegal.ucla.edu/)
UCLA INTERNATIONAL EDUCATION OFFICE
: B-300 MURPHY HALL, (310) 825-4995
Provides information about various study abroad programs, both UC and non-UC sponsored, including Education Abroad Program (EAP). (
http://www.ieo.ucla.edu
)
This is a partial list. Consult the UCLA Directory (
http://www.directory.ucla.edu
) to find other offices and services.
